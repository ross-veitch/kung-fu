# /annual-protocol — Design a comprehensive annual health protocol

**Purpose**: Build a structured 12-month health optimization plan covering diagnostics, interventions, monitoring, and lifestyle targets across all major longevity domains.

**When to use**: At the start of a new year, after a comprehensive lab panel, or when the patient wants a structured framework for health optimization rather than ad-hoc interventions.

---

## Phase 1: Baseline assessment

Before prescribing anything, establish the current state across all domains:

### Required diagnostics (complete before designing protocol)

**Bloodwork (comprehensive panel):**
- Metabolic: fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, HOMA-IR
- Lipids (advanced): ApoB, Lp(a), LDL-P (NMR), triglycerides, HDL, remnant cholesterol
- Inflammatory: hsCRP, homocysteine, ferritin
- Hormones: total/free testosterone, SHBG, DHEA-S, full thyroid (TSH, fT4, fT3, rT3, antibodies), estradiol, cortisol (AM)
- Nutrients: Vitamin D (25-OH), B12, folate, magnesium (RBC), omega-3 index
- Organ function: CBC, CMP (liver, kidney), GGT

**Body composition:**
- DEXA scan (visceral fat, lean mass by region, bone mineral density)

**Cardiovascular:**
- VO2 max test (metabolic cart preferred; Garmin/WHOOP estimate acceptable as proxy)
- Resting HR and HRV baseline (7-day average)
- CAC score (if ≥40 years old or family history of CVD)

**Functional:**
- Grip strength (dynamometer)
- Dead hang time
- Sit-to-stand from floor (no hands)

### Risk stratification

Based on results, classify risk in each domain:

| Domain | Low risk | Moderate risk | High risk |
|--------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Metabolic | Fasting insulin <5, HbA1c <5.4% | Insulin 5-10, HbA1c 5.4-5.6% | Insulin >10, HbA1c >5.7% |
| Cardiovascular | ApoB <80, CAC 0 | ApoB 80-120, CAC 1-100 | ApoB >120, CAC >100 |
| Body composition | Visceral fat low, lean mass adequate | Borderline visceral fat | High visceral fat, low lean mass |
| Hormonal | All within optimal ranges | 1-2 suboptimal markers | Multiple deficiencies |
| Fitness | VO2 max top 25% for age | 25th-50th percentile | Bottom 25% |

High-risk domains get priority intervention. Low-risk domains get maintenance protocols.

## Phase 2: Design the annual protocol

Structure the year in four quarters, each with a specific emphasis:

### Q1 (Jan–Mar): Foundation and diagnostics
**Focus**: Establish baselines, fix low-hanging fruit, build habits

- Complete all baseline diagnostics (bloodwork, DEXA, VO2 max)
- Address any acute deficiencies (Vitamin D, omega-3, magnesium)
- Establish exercise routine: Zone 2 base (3×/week, 30-45 min) + strength (2×/week)
- Sleep optimization audit: temperature, timing, light exposure, screen habits
- Begin daily tracking: HRV, sleep, body weight (weekly average)

### Q2 (Apr–Jun): Intervention ramp
**Focus**: Introduce targeted interventions based on Q1 findings

- Start any pharmaceutical interventions (statin if ApoB elevated, TRT if indicated, thyroid if needed)
- Ramp training: increase Zone 2 to 4×/week, add VO2 max intervals 1×/week (4×4 protocol)
- Nutrition intervention: if metabolic risk identified, implement time-restricted eating or CGM experiment (2-week wear)
- Supplement protocol stabilized (see Phase 3)
- Repeat key bloodwork markers at 12 weeks to assess intervention response

### Q3 (Jul–Sep): Optimization
**Focus**: Fine-tune based on Q2 data, push performance

- Review Q2 labs: are ApoB, HbA1c, inflammatory markers improving?
- Adjust doses or interventions based on response
- Peak fitness phase: highest training volume of the year
- Consider advanced interventions if appropriate (rapamycin, senolytics — evidence review required)
- Mental health check: stress levels, cognitive performance, mood

### Q4 (Oct–Dec): Review and plan
**Focus**: Comprehensive reassessment, plan next year

- Full repeat of baseline diagnostics (compare year-over-year)
- DEXA scan (body composition change)
- VO2 max retest
- Comprehensive bloodwork panel
- Review all interventions: what worked, what didn't, what to continue
- Design next year's protocol based on updated data

## Phase 3: Supplement and intervention stack

Prescribe by evidence tier:

### Tier 1: Strong evidence, broadly recommended
| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Rationale |
|-----------|------|--------|-----------|
| Vitamin D3 | 2,000–5,000 IU/day (titrate to 50-70 ng/mL) | Morning, with fat | Immune function, bone health, mood |
| Omega-3 (EPA+DHA) | 2–3g/day | With meals | Anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular |
| Magnesium glycinate | 400mg | Before bed | Sleep, muscle relaxation, 300+ enzymatic reactions |
| Creatine monohydrate | 5g/day | Any time | Cognitive function, muscle performance, neuroprotection |

### Tier 2: Good evidence, individual benefit varies
| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Rationale |
|-----------|------|--------|-----------|
| Vitamin K2 (MK-7) | 100–200 mcg/day | With Vitamin D | Calcium metabolism, arterial health |
| CoQ10 (ubiquinol) | 100–200mg/day | Morning | Mitochondrial function (especially if on statin) |
| Curcumin (with piperine) | 500mg/day | With meals | Anti-inflammatory |

### Tier 3: Emerging evidence, case-by-case
| Intervention | Protocol | Evidence level | Notes |
|-------------|----------|---------------|-------|
| NMN/NR | 500–1000mg NMN or 300mg NR daily | Moderate (animal strong, human emerging) | NAD+ precursor; monitor with labs |
| Rapamycin | 3–6mg weekly (intermittent) | Strong animal, early human | mTOR inhibition; requires physician oversight |
| Metformin | 500–1000mg/day | Moderate (TAME trial pending) | May interfere with exercise adaptation; consider timing |

**Rule**: Tier 1 for everyone. Tier 2 based on individual lab results. Tier 3 only with informed consent and physician monitoring.

## Phase 4: Monitoring cadence

### Weekly (automated via wearables)
- HRV 7-day average trend
- Sleep duration and efficiency
- Resting heart rate trend
- Training load and recovery balance

### Monthly
- Body weight (weekly average, track trend not daily fluctuation)
- Subjective wellness: energy, mood, cognitive sharpness (1-10 scale)
- Training progression: are Zone 2 durations extending? Are lifts progressing?

### Quarterly
- Targeted bloodwork: ApoB (if on intervention), fasting insulin, HbA1c, Vitamin D, testosterone (if on TRT)
- Adjust supplement doses based on results
- Review training programme: is VO2 max improving? Is strength progressing?

### Annually
- Full comprehensive bloodwork panel
- DEXA scan
- VO2 max test
- CAC score (if prior score >0, or every 3-5 years if 0)
- Functional tests (grip strength, dead hang, sit-to-stand)

## Phase 5: Lifestyle pillars (non-negotiable foundations)

No supplement or intervention compensates for failures in these four areas:

**Sleep**: 7–8.5 hours per night, consistent schedule (±30 min), cool room (65-68°F), dark, no screens 1h before bed. This is the single highest-leverage intervention for longevity.

**Exercise**: Minimum 150 min/week Zone 2 + 2 strength sessions. VO2 max is the strongest predictor of all-cause mortality. Strength prevents sarcopenia and falls. Both are non-negotiable.

**Nutrition**: Adequate protein (1.6g/kg/day minimum), emphasize whole foods, limit ultra-processed food, moderate alcohol (<7 drinks/week, ideally zero). No single diet wins — adherence and protein adequacy matter most.

**Stress management**: Chronic stress elevates cortisol, disrupts sleep, impairs immune function. Daily practice: meditation, breathwork, walking, or any deliberate downregulation. 10 minutes minimum.
