## 1. Key Insights (Updates to Prior Understanding)

**1. Lifelong low apoB does not increase dementia risk — it reduces it** *(The Drive, Feb 27)*
This is a meaningful update. The concern that aggressive lipid lowering harms cognition has been a persistent clinical hesitation, particularly with statins and now with PCSK9 inhibitors. A large genetic (Mendelian randomization) study showing the *opposite* directional effect — lower atherogenic lipoproteins → lower dementia risk — should shift the prior substantially. The genetic design largely sidesteps the "sick quitter" confounding that plagued observational statin-cognition studies. I can now counsel patients more confidently that aggressive apoB reduction is not a cognitive trade-off.

**2. Tyrosine, not just methionine, may matter for sex-specific longevity** *(Aging-US, March 1)*
The amino acid restriction literature has focused heavily on methionine. A causal signal linking higher blood tyrosine to ~1 year shorter male lifespan — with no effect in women — is genuinely novel and underappreciated. Mechanistically plausible (tyrosine feeds catecholamine and mTOR pathways). Too early to act on clinically, but this adds to the case for sex-specific protein/amino acid optimization rather than universal high-protein prescriptions. Worth flagging to male patients on aggressive protein supplementation protocols.

**3. Circadian disruption as a unified upstream risk factor** *(Biogerontology, Feb 17)*
Not a new idea, but the framing of circadian disruption as a single upstream driver of metabolic, neurodegenerative, cardiovascular, and immune aging simultaneously is a useful clinical organizing principle. Reinforces that sleep/light timing interventions aren't "soft" lifestyle advice — they're mechanistically upstream of multiple disease pathways simultaneously.

**4. Blood-borne factors from exercise may be as important as exercise itself** *(Huberman Lab / Wyss-Coray, Feb 23)*
The framing shift here is important: exercise isn't just mechanical stress on muscle and cardiovascular tissue — it generates circulating proteins (klotho, GDF11 and others) that act systemically on brain, heart, and other organs. This reframes why "enjoyable exercise you'll sustain" may outperform high-intensity protocols patients abandon: the systemic factor secretion requires *consistent* exposure, not peak intensity. Clinically actionable framing for patient adherence conversations.

**5. Unity Biotechnology's wind-down signals a senolytics field reset** *(Unity Biotechnology)*
UBX1325 (BCL-xL inhibitor) met a meaningful clinical bar — vision outcomes comparable to aflibercept — but failed primary endpoints and the company is liquidating. This is an intellectually honest signal that: (a) senolytic target validation in humans remains harder than preclinical data suggested, (b) BCL-xL inhibition specifically carries narrow therapeutic windows, and (c) the field is consolidating toward better-validated platforms. I should update patient conversations about senolytics from "promising pipeline" to "preclinical-to-clinical translation remains unproven; watch but don't recommend."

**6. OGTT outperforms HbA1c for early insulin resistance detection** *(The Drive, AMA #80, March 1)*
This isn't new from Attia, but its repetition in an AMA format suggests it's still underimplemented clinically. The practical gap: most physicians order HbA1c; OGTT with insulin levels at 1- and 2-hour marks catches insulin resistance a decade earlier. Worth reinforcing in my own practice protocols.

## 2. What's Changing (Early Signals, Not Yet Headlines)

**Signal 1: GLP-1 agonists repositioning toward geroprotection, not just metabolic disease**
Attia's AMA #80 explicitly discusses GLP-1s' "geroprotective potential" — this language is new and deliberate. The framing is shifting from "obesity drug" → "metabolic drug" → now "aging drug." This is early but directionally important. If GLP-1 receptor agonism has neuroprotective and systemic anti-inflammatory effects independent of weight loss, the indication logic changes significantly. Watch for trial data on GLP-1s in non-obese aging populations.

**Signal 2: Circulating blood protein profiling as an organ-specific aging clock**
Wyss-Coray's work on organs aging at different rates — measurable via blood biomarkers — is moving toward clinical utility. The implication: "biological age" will fragment into organ-specific ages, and interventions will be targeted accordingly. This is 3–5 years from routine clinical use but the conceptual framework is solidifying now.

**Signal 3: Senolytics field consolidation after Unity's failure**
The exit of Unity doesn't kill senolytics — it culls the weakest clinical translation. Remaining players (Oisín Biotechnologies, Cleara Biotech, dasatinib+quercetin research) will face higher bars. The field is quietly resetting expectations from "imminent clinical tools" to "longer development horizon." Physicians should stop citing Unity as evidence senolytics are clinically available.

**Signal 4: Sex-specific longevity interventions gaining mechanistic support**
The tyrosine/lifespan finding (male-specific) joins a growing pattern — fasting effects, protein requirements, and now amino acid metabolism showing sex-differentiated outcomes. The "one protocol fits all" approach to longevity optimization is increasingly untenable. This will likely become a more prominent clinical theme in 2026.

## 3. Ideas → Skills/Commands (Improvements to Expert Knowledge Files)

**Update: Lipid optimization command**
Add explicit note: genetic evidence now supports that lifelong low apoB reduces — not increases — dementia risk. Remove the hedge language about cognitive trade-offs from aggressive lipid lowering. Retain nuance about *acute* large LDL drops in elderly (different mechanism) but update the chronic/genetic framing.

**Update: Insulin resistance screening protocol**
Formalize OGTT + fasting insulin + 1hr/2hr insulin as the standard early detection protocol in the knowledge base, explicitly noting HbA1c misses early IR by ~10 years. Add specific cutoffs: 1-hour glucose >155 mg/dL as a meaningful threshold.

**Update: Exercise prescription framing**
Add the "circulating exercise factors" mechanism (klotho, GDF11, etc.) as a rationale for consistency over intensity. Update patient communication templates to emphasize that the systemic benefits of exercise are partly mediated by blood-borne factors requiring regular exposure — not just acute cardiovascular stress.

**Update: Senolytics section**
Downgrade Unity/BCL-xL inhibition from "promising pipeline" to "clinical translation failed primary endpoints; field resetting." Note that dasatinib+quercetin remains the only human-tested senolytic combination with any clinical data, and even that evidence base is thin.

**New addition: Sex-specific protein/amino acid optimization**
Create a note flagging emerging evidence that amino acid profiles (tyrosine specifically) may have sex-differentiated longevity effects. Flag as "monitor, not yet actionable" but begin tracking this literature thread.
