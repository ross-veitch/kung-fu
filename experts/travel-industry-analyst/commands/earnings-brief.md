# /earnings-brief

Synthesise an OTA, airline, or hotel company's quarterly earnings into strategic implications.

---

## When to use

After a key travel company reports quarterly earnings — to extract competitive intelligence, market signals, and strategic implications relevant to your company.

---

## Steps

### 1. Source the materials
- Earnings press release (IR page or SEC EDGAR for public companies)
- Earnings call transcript (Seeking Alpha, The Motley Fool, or Bloomberg for full transcripts)
- Investor presentation / earnings deck (usually posted to IR page)
- Prior quarter transcript (for context on what they guided and whether they delivered)

### 2. Extract the key metrics

**For OTAs** (Booking Holdings, Expedia, Airbnb, Trip.com):
- Room nights / bookings (growth YoY and QoQ)
- Gross booking value (GBV) and growth
- Take rate (Revenue / GBV) — expanding or compressing?
- Revenue growth
- Adjusted EBITDA / margin
- Marketing spend as % of revenue (efficiency signal)
- Direct / loyalty mix (branded traffic reducing paid dependency?)
- Geographic mix (which regions growing fastest?)
- Alternative accommodation share (Booking.com vs Airbnb share of pie)

**For airlines**:
- RPK growth (traffic volume)
- RASM (Revenue per Available Seat Mile) — yield trend
- Load factor
- CASM (Cost per ASM) — cost efficiency
- Ancillary revenue per passenger
- Forward bookings / advance load factor
- Capacity plans (ASK growth guidance)
- Fuel cost and hedging position

**For hotel companies**:
- RevPAR growth (same-store — most important)
- ADR and occupancy trends
- Net unit growth (new rooms added to system)
- Fee revenue growth (if asset-light / franchise model)
- Pipeline (rooms under construction — future unit growth)
- Direct booking mix (loyalty channel %)

### 3. Listen for forward guidance signals

Forward guidance is more valuable than backward results. Note:
- What demand trends are they seeing in the current quarter?
- What's the booking window doing? (Shorter or longer)
- Are they expanding or pulling back marketing spend?
- Any commentary on geographic shifts (MENA, APAC, Europe?)?
- What are they worried about? (Listen to what they emphasise in risk factors)
- What's the tone? Confident or hedging?

### 4. Q&A analysis

The prepared remarks are polished. The Q&A reveals more:
- Which questions did management deflect or answer vaguely?
- Where did they get the most detailed, confident answers?
- What did analysts probe hardest? (Signals where the market sees risk)
- Any major strategic questions that weren't asked that should have been?

### 5. Strategic implications

For each major finding, assess implications:
- **For your company**: Does this create opportunity or threat?
- **For the industry**: What does this signal about overall market health?
- **Competitive dynamics**: Does this change your competitive strategy?
