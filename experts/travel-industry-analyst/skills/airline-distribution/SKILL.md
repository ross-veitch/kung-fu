# Airline Distribution

## GDS (Global Distribution Systems)

**The Big Three**:
- Sabre (US-based, ~35% market share)
- Amadeus (Europe-based, ~40% market share)
- Travelport (Worldspan + Galileo, ~20% market share)

**How GDS works**:
1. Airlines load fares/inventory into GDS
2. Travel agents (OTAs, TMCs) search GDS for availability
3. Agent books ticket through GDS
4. GDS charges airline $10-15 per booking segment

**GDS revenue model**: Transaction fees from airlines + booking fees from travel agents

**Why airlines dislike GDS**:
- High cost ($10-15/segment adds up at scale)
- Limited merchandising (can't show seat maps, baggage options dynamically)
- Loss of customer data (GDS owns the relationship)

---

## NDC (New Distribution Capability)

**What it is**: IATA standard for airlines to distribute content via APIs (not legacy GDS protocols)

**Benefits for airlines**:
- Lower distribution cost (~$2/booking vs $10-15 for GDS)
- Better merchandising (show seat maps, ancillary upsells, dynamic offers)
- Own customer data

**Adoption status (2024)**:
- ~30% of airline bookings globally flow through NDC
- Full-service carriers (Lufthansa, United, Emirates) pushing hard
- Low-cost carriers (Ryanair, Southwest) were NDC-native from day one

**Barriers to adoption**:
- OTAs/TMCs slow to integrate (requires tech investment)
- Corporate travel programs tied to GDS (hard to switch)
- Fare parity issues (NDC-only fares create complexity)

**Winners**: Airlines (cost savings), tech-forward OTAs (differentiated content)  
**Losers**: GDS companies (lose transaction volume), legacy TMCs (struggle to adapt)

## Ancillary Revenue

**Ancillary categories**:
- **Baggage fees**: Checked bag, carry-on (LCCs), overweight fees
- **Seat selection**: Extra legroom, window/aisle, advance selection
- **In-flight services**: Meals, Wi-Fi, entertainment
- **Lounge access, priority boarding, fast-track security**

**Benchmark**: LCCs generate 30-50% of revenue from ancillaries (Ryanair ~50%, Spirit 45%). Full-service carriers: 10-20%.

**NDC advantage**: Can dynamically price ancillaries based on demand, user profile, booking context.

## Airline Distribution Cost Breakdown

| Channel | Cost per Booking | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Direct (airline.com) | $2-5 | Cheapest, but requires marketing spend to drive traffic |
| NDC | ~$2-3 | Low cost, but requires OTA integration |
| GDS | $10-15 | Expensive, but reaches TMCs and OTAs at scale |
| Metasearch (Google Flights) | $5-10 CPC | Variable, depends on bidding |

**Strategy**: Push customers to direct (lowest cost), use NDC for OTAs, maintain GDS for corporate/TMC.
