# OTA & Metasearch

## OTA Business Models

**Booking Holdings** (Booking.com, Priceline, Agoda, Kayak):
- Market cap: ~$140B (2024)
- Model: Marketplace (connects travelers with suppliers)
- Take rate: ~15%
- Strategy: Own the customer, Genius loyalty programme, invest in direct traffic (SEO/SEM)

**Expedia Group** (Expedia, Hotels.com, Vrbo):
- Market cap: ~$20B (2024)
- Model: Hybrid (marketplace + owned supply via Vrbo)
- Take rate: ~15-18%
- Strategy: Leverage scale, cross-sell (flight + hotel packages), loyalty (One Key)

**Airbnb**:
- Market cap: ~$80B (2024)
- Model: Marketplace (unique supply: homes, not hotels)
- Take rate: ~13-15% (guest service fee + host fee)
- Moat: Supply side (millions of unique listings)

## Metasearch Economics

**How metasearch works**:
1. User searches for "hotels in Dubai"
2. Metasearch (Google, TripAdvisor) shows results
3. OTAs/hotels bid CPC (cost-per-click) to show up
4. User clicks → goes to OTA → books

**Metasearch players**:
- **Google Hotel Ads**: Largest (60%+ of metasearch traffic)
- **TripAdvisor**: Strong brand, but losing share to Google
- **trivago**: Owned by Expedia (conflict of interest)
- **Kayak**: Owned by Booking Holdings

**CPC benchmarks**:
- Google Hotel Ads: $1-5 CPC (varies by market, competition)
- TripAdvisor: $0.50-3 CPC

**Incrementality question**: Does metasearch drive NEW bookings, or cannibalize direct traffic? Answer: Varies by OTA. Booking.com has strong direct brand → metasearch is more cannibalistic. Smaller OTAs rely more on metasearch.

## Google's Expanding Role

**Phase 1**: Metasearch (send traffic to OTAs, charge CPC)  
**Phase 2**: Direct booking (Google becomes the OTA, keeps the booking fee)

**2024 status**: Google offers direct booking for hotels in some markets (US, Europe). Threat to OTAs.

**OTA response**: Invest in brand (reduce reliance on Google), negotiate better terms, diversify traffic sources.

---

## Rate Parity (MFN Clauses)

**Most-Favored-Nation (MFN)**: Hotel agrees to show same or better rate on OTA vs hotel's direct site.

**EU ruling (2019)**: MFN clauses deemed anti-competitive in some jurisdictions → hotels can offer cheaper rates on direct sites.

**Impact**: Hotels can now undercut OTAs with direct rates → OTAs lose margin advantage.

## Loyalty Programmes

**Booking.com Genius**: 3 tiers (levels 1-3), discounts up to 15%, free breakfast/room upgrades.  
**Expedia One Key**: Points system, earn 2× points on Expedia bookings.  
**Airbnb**: No traditional loyalty programme (host quality is the "loyalty")

**Economics**: Loyalty members have 2× booking frequency and 30% higher LTV → worth investing in retention.
