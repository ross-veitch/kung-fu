# Travel Market Intelligence

How to read and synthesise travel industry data — earnings calls, OAG schedules, STR reports, IATA statistics, and competitive analysis.

---

## Earnings calls

The highest-signal source of competitive intelligence. Public travel companies report quarterly and share detailed metrics, strategic priorities, and forward guidance.

### Key companies to track

**OTAs**: Booking Holdings (BKNG), Expedia Group (EXPE), Airbnb (ABNB), Trip.com Group (TCOM)
**Airlines**: Emirates (private but publishes annual results), Etihad (private), Qatar Airways (private), Ryanair, Delta, United
**Hotels**: Marriott (MAR), Hilton (HLT), IHG, Accor
**GDS/Tech**: Amadeus (AMS.MC), Sabre (SABR), Travelport (private)

### What to listen for

- **Room nights / bookings growth**: Volume indicator. Separate organic growth from acquisitions.
- **ADR / RevPAR / Take rate trends**: Pricing power indicator. Especially important for OTAs — is take rate expanding or compressing?
- **Direct mix / alternative accommodation mix**: Strategic direction. Booking.com's alternative accommodation share is a key competitive metric.
- **Marketing efficiency**: Cost per booking, return on ad spend. Are they spending more or less to acquire the same customer?
- **Geographic mix**: Which regions are growing? MENA and APAC growth rates vs. Europe and NA.
- **Forward guidance**: Management commentary on booking trends for next quarter. This is leading data.
- **Capital allocation**: Share buybacks, M&A plans, capex. Reveals strategic priorities.

### Reading transcripts

Don't just read the prepared remarks — the Q&A is where analysts probe weak spots. Management's tone and specificity in Q&A answers reveals what they're worried about. Watch for: deflected questions, vague answers to specific questions, repeated use of "headwinds."

## OAG data

The most complete airline schedule database. OAG captures planned flights, seat counts, frequencies, and aircraft types for virtually every commercial airline globally.

**Use cases**:
- **Capacity analysis**: Total seats on a route or in a market over time. Capacity additions signal airline confidence in demand.
- **Route analytics**: New routes announced, routes cut, frequency changes. Track week-over-week or month-over-month for trends.
- **Competitive dynamics**: Seat share by airline on a route. Who's growing? Who's retrenching?
- **Connectivity analysis**: Connecting traffic flows through hubs. Critical for understanding Gulf carrier strategy (DXB, DOH, AUH as global hubs).

**Caveat**: OAG shows planned/scheduled capacity, not actual operated capacity. Airlines can and do cancel flights without always updating schedules promptly.

## STR data

Smith Travel Research — the universal benchmarking standard for hotels.

**Key reports**:
- **STAR report**: Your property vs. comp set on occupancy, ADR, RevPAR. Monthly and annual. The most used hotel performance report globally.
- **Pipeline report**: Hotels under construction or in planning. Forward supply indicator.
- **Destination report**: Market-level data (city or region). Useful for sizing total addressable market.

**Metrics**:
- **Occupancy index**: Your occupancy / comp set occupancy × 100. >100 = outperforming.
- **ADR index**: Same formula for rate. >100 = premium pricing.
- **RevPAR index**: The combined measure. This is what matters most — it captures both rate and occupancy.

## IATA statistics

IATA publishes comprehensive air transport data:
- **World Air Transport Statistics (WATS)**: Annual compilation — RPKs (Revenue Passenger Kilometers), freight tonnes, airline financials
- **Monthly traffic statistics**: RPK growth by region, load factors
- **Economic performance of the airline industry**: Annual forecast of airline profitability, revenue, costs

**Key metrics**:
- **RPK** (Revenue Passenger Kilometers): Passengers × distance flown. The standard measure of airline demand.
- **ASK** (Available Seat Kilometers): Seats × distance. The supply measure.
- **Load factor**: RPK / ASK. How full are planes? Pre-COVID norm was ~82%. Post-COVID recovery saw 80%+ by 2023.
- **Yield**: Revenue per RPK. The airline equivalent of ADR — are fares going up or down?

## Competitive analysis framework

For any travel company analysis, cover these dimensions systematically:

### 1. Business model clarity
- How does the company make money? (Commission, merchant, subscription, advertising, hybrid)
- What's the take rate? Is it expanding or compressing?
- What's the unit economics story? (CAC, LTV, contribution margin)

### 2. Demand positioning
- What traffic does the company control? (Brand, SEO, paid, app, direct type-in)
- How dependent are they on Google? (SEM spend as % of revenue)
- What's the repeat rate? (Returning users as % of bookings)

### 3. Supply positioning
- How much inventory do they have? (Hotels, airlines, activities connected)
- Exclusive inventory or commoditised? (Same hotels as everyone else?)
- Supplier relationship quality (commission negotiation power, content quality, rate competitiveness)

### 4. Geographic and segment mix
- Where are they strongest? Weakest?
- What segments (leisure vs business, domestic vs international, budget vs luxury)?
- Which markets are growing vs shrinking?

### 5. Technology and product
- App quality, search quality, booking flow
- Personalisation sophistication
- Mobile share of bookings
- AI/LLM strategy

### 6. Financial health
- Revenue growth, profitability trajectory
- Cash flow generation
- Debt levels and maturity
- Capital allocation decisions
