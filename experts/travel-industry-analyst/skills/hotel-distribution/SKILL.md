# Hotel Distribution

How hotels reach consumers — OTA commissions, direct booking strategies, channel manager integrations, metasearch, rate parity, and the economics of each channel.

---

## Distribution channels

### OTA (Online Travel Agencies)

The dominant digital distribution channel for hotels. Booking.com, Expedia, Agoda, Trip.com.

**Commission model**: Hotels pay 15–25% of booking value. Rate varies by market, property size, and negotiated terms. Preferred partner programs (Booking.com Preferred, Expedia Preferred) offer better placement in exchange for higher commission or rate parity guarantees.

**Net rate model**: OTA buys room at wholesale rate, sells to consumer at marked-up rate. OTA controls consumer pricing. More common with Expedia (historically merchant-heavy) and Agoda.

**Value proposition to hotels**: Demand aggregation (the hotel can't reach these customers alone), technology (booking engine, payment processing, reviews), and marketing (SEO, SEM, brand).

### Direct channels

Hotel's own website, call centre, walk-ins, loyalty programme direct bookings.

**Direct booking incentive**: Major chains (Marriott, Hilton, IHG) have invested heavily in driving direct bookings — loyalty programme perks, best rate guarantees, exclusive member prices. The "Book Direct" movement has meaningfully shifted share from OTAs to direct, particularly for branded chains.

**Economics**: Direct bookings cost hotels roughly 5–12% when including website maintenance, SEM/SEO, loyalty costs, and payment processing. Significantly cheaper than OTA commissions but requires brand strength and marketing investment.

### GDS (Global Distribution Systems)

Sabre, Amadeus, Travelport. Primarily used for corporate/business travel bookings through TMCs (Travel Management Companies). Higher commission (18–25% including TMC fees) but valuable high-ADR business traveler segment.

### Metasearch

Google Hotel Ads, TripAdvisor, Trivago, Kayak. Hotels bid on CPC basis for placement. Effective acquisition cost varies wildly (2–15%) depending on bidding strategy and conversion rate.

### Wholesale / tour operators

Traditional B2B distribution — hotels provide net rates to wholesalers who package with flights. Particularly important in MENA (group travel, Umrah packages) and APAC (package tour culture).

## Rate parity

One of the most contested issues in hotel distribution.

**What it is**: Contractual requirement that hotels offer the same rate on OTA as on their own website. Two types:
- **Wide parity**: Same rate everywhere (OTA, direct, all channels). Increasingly banned in Europe.
- **Narrow parity**: Hotel can't offer lower rate on its own website, but other OTAs and offline channels can differ.

**Regulatory status**: Banned or restricted in France, Germany, Italy, Austria, Belgium, and other EU markets (following antitrust investigations into Booking.com). Still enforced in much of MENA, APAC, and Americas.

**Impact**: Rate parity limits hotels' ability to incentivise direct bookings on price. Hotels work around it via loyalty member rates, package bundles, and opaque channels.

## Channel managers

Technology that connects a hotel's PMS (Property Management System) to multiple distribution channels simultaneously.

**Key players**: SiteMinder (the market leader for independent hotels), D-EDGE (Accor ecosystem), RateGain, Cloudbeds (PMS + channel manager combo), Hotelogix.

**What they do**: Push rate and availability updates to all connected channels in real-time, pull bookings back into the PMS, prevent overbooking (inventory sync).

**Why they matter**: Without a channel manager, hotels manually update rates on each OTA extranet — slow, error-prone, and impossible to do dynamic pricing at scale. Channel managers are the infrastructure layer that makes multi-channel distribution work.

## Key metrics

| Metric | Definition | Benchmark guidance |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **ADR** (Average Daily Rate) | Total room revenue / Rooms sold | Market-specific; STR provides benchmarks |
| **Occupancy** | Rooms sold / Rooms available | 60–75% is typical; varies by market and segment |
| **RevPAR** | Revenue per Available Room = ADR × Occupancy | The universal performance metric; STR comp set is the benchmark |
| **Channel mix** | % of bookings from each channel (OTA vs direct vs GDS vs wholesale) | Healthy: 40–60% direct for branded chains, 60–80% OTA for independents |
| **CPA by channel** | Cost per acquisition by distribution channel | Direct: 5–12%, OTA: 15–25%, GDS: 18–25%, Metasearch: 2–15% |
| **Cancellation rate by channel** | % of bookings cancelled before stay | OTA free-cancellation rates are typically 30–40%+ higher than direct |

## STR (Smith Travel Research)

The gold standard for hotel competitive benchmarking. Hotels submit their data and receive competitive set (comp set) reports showing how they perform relative to selected competitors.

**STR STAR report**: Shows your property's ADR, Occupancy, and RevPAR indexed against your comp set. Index >100 means outperforming. The most important report in hotel revenue management.
