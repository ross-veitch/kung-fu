# /backcast — Work Backwards from a Desired Future

Start with a specific, named future state and work backwards to today. Identify the critical path, decision points, and earliest moments to act. The complement to forward projection — where `/10yr` asks "where will we be?", `/backcast` asks "what would have to be true to get *there*?"

---

## When to use

- You have a clear strategic vision and want to understand the critical path to get there
- Before committing to a major investment: "If this bet pays off in 2030, what had to happen by 2027? 2025?"
- To surface hidden dependencies: things that must be true earlier than most people assume
- To identify decision deadlines: the last responsible moment to make each commitment

---

## Input required

1. **The desired future state** — be specific. Not "Wego is a big AI company" but "Wego is the default AI-native travel planning layer for 200M MENA users, with agents from EK, WY, SQ, and the top 20 MENA hotels embedded in Wego's agent ecosystem."
2. **The target year** — when does this future state materialise? (e.g., 2030, 2032, 2035)
3. **Current year baseline** — what is the current state we're working backwards from? (default: today)

---

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Anchor the Vision

Before working backwards, sharpen the input. A good backcast target has:
- **Observable indicators**: you'll know when you've arrived because X, Y, Z are measurable
- **Named actors**: which customers, suppliers, partners, or regulators are doing what?
- **Named capabilities**: what can the company do at the target date that it cannot do today?

If the input is vague, sharpen it before proceeding.

### Phase 2: The Backcast Ladder

Work backwards in 2–3 year intervals from the target to today.

For each interval, answer:
1. **What must be true by [year]?** — specific preconditions for the next phase
2. **What must the company have built/won/proven?** — capabilities, partnerships, market position
3. **What must the external environment have done?** — market development, regulatory conditions, technology maturity
4. **What must NOT have happened?** — foreclosed paths, lock-ins, competitive preemptions

### Phase 3: Critical Path Identification

Across all intervals, identify:

**Critical path nodes** — conditions that must occur on time for the sequence to hold. Missing one delays everything downstream.

**Float nodes** — conditions that matter but have slack; can be delayed 1–2 intervals without breaking the chain.

**Parallel tracks** — conditions that can be worked simultaneously (dependency-free).

**External dependencies** — things the company cannot control but must happen anyway (technology maturity, market conditions, regulatory approvals). Flag these explicitly.

### Phase 4: Decision Deadlines

For each major decision or investment:
- **Latest responsible commitment date** — by when must this be decided? Waiting longer forecloses the option.
- **Earliest viable commitment date** — before this, committing is premature (technology not mature, market not legible)
- **Trigger signals** — what observable signals would cause you to move earlier or later?

### Phase 5: Today's Actions

Given the backcast:
- **Must start now**: actions with long lead times (capability building, relationship development, R&D) where delay compounds
- **Watch and prepare**: things to monitor closely and be ready to act on quickly
- **Deliberate non-decisions**: things to explicitly *not* commit to yet, to preserve optionality

---

## Notes

- The backcast often reveals that "we need to start X immediately" even when X seems premature from a forward-looking view. This is the point of the exercise.
- External dependencies (technology readiness, regulatory environment) are the most common blind spots. Give them disproportionate attention.
- For Wego specifically: the "when do agents replace search?" question is almost always a critical path node. Force it to be named and dated.
- If the critical path seems impossibly tight: that is also signal. Either the vision needs to move to a later year, or the strategy needs a step-change move (acquisition, partnership) rather than organic build.
