# /future-landscape — Sector Landscape at Future Horizon

**Purpose**: Map what a sector looks like at a specific future horizon — competitive structure, business model dominance, who survived, who was created, and what the customer experience looks like.

**When to use**: Competitive strategy development, investment thesis, understanding the end state to work backwards from.

---

## Phase 1: Define the sector and horizon

State:
- Sector (be specific: not "travel" but "OTA-mediated hotel booking" or "airline distribution")
- Horizon: 5yr / 10yr
- Lens: competitive structure? Business model? Consumer behaviour? All three?

---

## Phase 2: Current landscape baseline

Map today's landscape:
- Market structure (concentrated vs fragmented, platform vs linear)
- Dominant business models and their economics
- Value chain: who captures margin at each step?
- Technology regime: what technology is the current competitive foundation?
- Regulatory regime: what rules shape competition today?

---

## Phase 3: Forces of change

Identify the 3–5 forces most likely to reshape the landscape:
- Technology shifts (what technology makes the current model obsolete or newly viable?)
- Business model innovation (what new economic structure could outcompete the current one?)
- Consumer behaviour shifts (what do customers in 5–10 years want that today's customers don't?)
- Regulatory changes (what rules are coming that restructure the playing field?)
- Geopolitical shifts (what market access, ownership, or data sovereignty changes are coming?)

---

## Phase 4: Build the future landscape

Construct a picture of the sector at the target horizon:

**Market structure**: more concentrated or more fragmented than today? Why?
- Forces of consolidation: scale economies, network effects, capital requirements
- Forces of fragmentation: technology democratisation, regulatory intervention, niche specialisation

**Business model dominance**: what economic models win at this horizon?
- Which models have improving unit economics as technology evolves?
- Which models have deteriorating unit economics?

**Value chain**: where does margin concentrate?
- Typically: control points shift with technology regime changes

**Competitive dynamics**:
- Which current incumbents survive? Why?
- Which current incumbents are disrupted? By what mechanism?
- What new entrant archetypes emerge (from what adjacent sectors, with what structural advantages)?
- What M&A patterns should we expect (consolidation in which layers)?

**Customer experience**: what does the end customer experience look like?
- Discovery, purchase, consumption, support at the target horizon

---

## Phase 5: Strategic implications

From this future landscape:
- Where should the company position to be on the winning side of structural change?
- What capabilities must be built before the landscape reaches this state?
- What current investments are being made in a layer that will lose margin?

---

## Output format

```
SECTOR: [specific sector]
HORIZON: [5yr / 10yr]

  Market structure: [concentrated/fragmented, platform/linear]
  Dominant business model: [description]
  Margin capture: [where in value chain]
  Technology foundation: [current regime]

FORCES OF CHANGE:
  1. [force] → [mechanism of change]
  2. ...

FUTURE LANDSCAPE (base case):
  Market structure: [vs today]
  Dominant business model: [what wins]
  Margin capture: [where shifts to]
  Technology foundation: [new regime]
  
  Winners (archetypes): [who thrives and why]
  Losers (archetypes): [who gets disrupted and how]
  New entrant threat: [from where, with what advantage]

STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS:
  [what this means for positioning today]

12–24 MONTH ACTIONS:
  1. ...
```
