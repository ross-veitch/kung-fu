# /10yr — 10-Year Horizon Analysis

**Purpose**: Project a company, industry, or technology trajectory to a 10-year horizon. Identify what is structurally inevitable, what is genuinely uncertain, and what it means for strategy today.

**When to use**: Annual planning, investment thesis development, entering a new market, stress-testing a long-term strategy.

---

## Phase 1: Establish baseline trajectory

Describe the current state clearly:
- What are the dominant dynamics today (business model, competitive structure, technology regime, regulatory environment)?
- What are the growth rates, margins, and capital flows in this sector?
- What does the consensus 3-year view look like?

*The 10-year analysis starts by making the short-term consensus explicit — then looking past it.*

---

## Phase 2: Identify structural forces (predetermined elements)

Some things will be true in 10 years regardless of what decisions are made now. Identify them:

**Technology S-curves**: where is the current technology on its S-curve? Early (still accelerating) → middle (standards emerging) → late (commoditizing)? Technologies in early phase will transform the sector. Technologies in late phase will be infrastructure.

**Demographic waves**: population age structures are effectively locked in for 10 years. What does the 2035 consumer cohort look like vs today's? Where is urbanisation heading?

**Energy transitions**: the shift to electrification, the cost curve of solar/batteries, and the geography of energy surplus/deficit are largely locked in.

**Geopolitical realignment**: the direction of US-China decoupling, EU regulatory expansion, and emerging market growth poles can be reasonably projected over 10 years.

---

## Phase 3: Map the key uncertainties

For the 2–4 factors that are genuinely uncertain over 10 years:
- What are the two poles of each uncertainty?
- What would cause each pole to be realised?
- What is the rough probability distribution (not precise, just directional)?

---

## Phase 4: Apply Carlota Perez techno-economic paradigm

Where are we in the current technological paradigm?

| Phase | Characteristics | Strategic implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Installation period** (irruption → frenzy) | New technology deployed; capital floods in; incumbents disrupted | Move fast; technology is the moat; establish position |
| **Turning point** (crash/reconfiguration) | Financial crash or regulatory reset; shake-out | Survive; acquire distressed assets; build sustainable models |
| **Deployment period** (synergy → maturity) | Technology becomes infrastructure; application layer flourishes; scale advantages dominate | Operate efficiently; win on distribution and data; application >> infrastructure |

For AI specifically (2024 context): likely mid-Installation period (Frenzy phase). Infrastructure (chips, models, cloud) still dominant but application layer maturing fast. A Turning Point (financial or regulatory) remains likely before full golden age Deployment.

---

## Phase 5: Build the 10-year picture

Synthesise into a coherent 10-year narrative:
- What does the industry look like in 2034–2035?
- Who are the dominant players, and why?
- What does the competitive structure look like (fragmented, consolidated, platform-dominated)?
- What happened to the current incumbents?
- What are consumers doing differently?

Write this as a vivid paragraph — not bullet points. The goal is to make the future feel real.

---

## Phase 6: Backcast to today

From the 10-year picture, work backwards:
- What capabilities must be built by year 5 to be competitive in year 10?
- What must be invested in (or divested from) in the next 2 years?
- What early decisions, if made wrong, would be hard to reverse?
