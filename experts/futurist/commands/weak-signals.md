# /weak-signals — Early Signal Detection

**Purpose**: Identify and prioritise early indicators of structural change in a domain before the shift becomes consensus. Most powerful when the signals are anomalous, early, and triangulated across independent sources.

**When to use**: Environmental scanning; identifying inflection points before competitors; quarterly foresight reviews.

---

## What makes a signal weak (and worth tracking)

A weak signal is:
- **Early**: most people haven't noticed it yet, or dismiss it as an anomaly
- **Directional**: points toward a structural change, not just a fluctuation
- **Triangulated**: corroborated by independent signals in adjacent domains

Noise masquerades as signal constantly. The discipline is filtering.

**Noise characteristics**: one-off, can be explained by cyclical dynamics, contradicted by other signals, driven by hype rather than underlying mechanism.

**Signal characteristics**: unexpected, hard to explain away, consistent with a broader structural story, appearing in multiple independent domains simultaneously.

---

## Phase 1: STEEP scan

Scan each domain for anomalies — things that don't fit the current dominant model.

**S — Social / behavioural**
What consumer or workforce behaviours are changing in ways the dominant model doesn't explain?
- Examples: unexpected generational shifts, new forms of status signalling, behavioural changes in emerging markets that precede developed market shifts

**T — Technology**
What's happening at the frontier that hasn't hit mainstream awareness yet?
- Examples: research papers that are 3–5 years from product, cost curves crossing thresholds, unexpected capability jumps

**E — Economic**
What economic structures are under pressure that haven't broken yet?
- Examples: margin structures that worked at lower interest rates, business models dependent on conditions that are shifting

**E — Environmental / physical**
What physical constraints or changes are being underestimated?
- Examples: energy cost curves, water stress in key geographies, climate policy lagging physical reality

**P — Political / regulatory**
Where is regulation heading that industry is not yet pricing in?
- Examples: regulatory signals from leading jurisdictions (EU → global), political economy shifts

---

## Phase 2: Signal prioritisation

For each signal identified, rate:
- **Impact if signal is correct**: how much does this change the strategic landscape? (1–5)
- **Confidence that signal is real**: how well-evidenced, how triangulated? (1–5)
- **Time to mainstream**: how long before this is consensus? (months / 1–2 years / 3–5 years / 5+ years)

Prioritise: High impact × medium-high confidence × 1–5 year time horizon.

**Discard**: low confidence, short time horizon (already mainstream), low impact.

---

## Phase 3: Implication mapping

For the top 5 signals:
- **First-order implication**: what directly changes if this signal is correct?
- **Second-order implication**: what changes *because* of the first-order change?
- **Strategic exposure**: which parts of the current strategy are most exposed?
- **Monitoring metric**: what single number or observable would confirm this signal is strengthening?

---

## Output format

```
DOMAIN: [sector / question]

TOP SIGNALS:

  [Signal name]
  What: [1–2 sentence description]
  Evidence: [where this signal is visible]
  Impact: [X/5] | Confidence: [X/5] | Time to mainstream: [estimate]
  First-order: [implication if true]
  Second-order: [what changes because of first-order]
  Exposure: [what part of current strategy is at risk]
  Monitor: [specific metric or indicator to watch]

  [repeat × 5]

NOISE (dismissed and why):
  - [Signal]: [reason it's noise, not signal]

SIGNALS MONITORING DASHBOARD:
  [Signal] → [metric] → [threshold that confirms strengthening]
```
