# /scenarios — Futures Analysis & Scenario Planning

Two modes depending on your starting point:

- **`/scenarios explore`** — broad scan first (STEEPV); good when you don't yet know which uncertainties matter
- **`/scenarios plan`** — structured 2×2 scenario matrix; good when the key uncertainties are known

Default (no flag): if the focal question is clear, go straight to `plan`. If it's exploratory, start with `explore`.

---

## MODE 1: `/scenarios explore` — Full Futures Scan

**Purpose**: Comprehensive scan of a topic, sector, or strategic question before committing to scenario axes. Surfaces structural forces, emerging signals, and the scenario space before zooming in.

**When to use**: Annual planning horizon scan, entering a new domain, or when you need the full picture before picking scenario axes.

### STEEPV framework scan

Run each lens systematically. Goal: surface structural forces, not just trends.

**S — Social & behavioural**
- Demographic shifts (ageing, urbanisation, household formation, workforce composition)
- Value shifts (what the next consumer cohort prioritises vs the current one)
- Behavioural changes enabled by technology (what becomes effortless that was effortful)
- Trust dynamics (who do people trust, and how is that shifting?)

**T — Technology**
- Where are the major capability S-curves? (AI, biotech, energy, materials, space)
- What is moving from experiment to deployment in the 2–5 year window?
- What cost curves are crossing thresholds? (compute, genome sequencing, solar, battery storage)
- What convergences are appearing? (AI × biology, AI × robotics, energy × mobility)

**E — Economic**
- Productivity wave: installation vs deployment phase of current paradigm?
- Debt cycle position: where are major economies in the long-term debt cycle?
- Capital flows: what sectors are capital-attracting vs capital-fleeing?
- Geopolitical economics: supply chain reshoring, sanctions regimes, digital payments fragmentation

**E — Environmental & physical**
- Climate: physical risk timeline; transition risk timeline (policy, stranded assets, insurance)
- Energy: cost of clean energy generation vs incumbents; timeline to parity by geography
- Resource constraints: water, rare earth minerals, arable land — where do these bite?

**P — Political & regulatory**
- Regulatory waves: what areas are moving from permissive to restrictive?
- Geopolitical realignment: US-China decoupling, EU as regulatory superpower, MENA strategic reorientation
- Democratic stability: where is political risk elevated?

**V — Values & worldview**
- What does the rising generation believe that current leadership does not?
- How are concepts of work, ownership, privacy, and trust evolving?
- What institutional legitimacy is eroding?

### Structural forces synthesis

From the scan, identify the 3–5 forces that are structural (not cyclical), high impact, and either predetermined or critically uncertain.

### Scenario space

- Most probable trajectory (if current forces continue)
- Most disruptive trajectory (if the biggest uncertainty resolves adversely)
- Most optimistic trajectory (if the biggest uncertainty resolves favourably)

### Output: six-element structure

1. **Structural shift**: what is changing at a deep level
2. **Second-order effects**: what changes as a result that most haven't noticed
3. **Strategic implications**: what this means for the organisation
4. **Breakpoints**: what would change this view substantially
5. **12–24 month actions**: what to do now
6. **Signals to monitor**: early indicators of which trajectory is materialising

## MODE 2: `/scenarios plan` — Structured 2×2 Scenario Matrix

**Purpose**: Build a structured 2×2 scenario matrix for a high-uncertainty strategic question. Generate 4 named, vivid, internally consistent future worlds and derive differentiated strategic implications from each.

**When to use**: Strategic planning under high uncertainty; capital allocation decisions; pre-wiring leadership to recognise futures as they emerge.

**Method base**: Shell scenario planning, adapted for company-level strategy.

### Phase 1: Define the focal question

State the strategic question the scenarios will inform. Be specific:
- ❌ "What does the future of travel look like?"
- ✅ "How should we allocate product investment between AI-native discovery and traditional search, given uncertainty about how AI reshapes OTA distribution over the next 5 years?"

### Phase 2: Identify driving forces

Scan across: **Technology / Economics / Social behaviour / Regulation / Geopolitics / Environment**

Generate 10–15 forces that could materially affect the focal question. For each, rate:
- **Impact** on the focal question: High / Medium / Low
- **Certainty**: Predetermined (will happen regardless) vs Uncertain (could go either way)

Separate:
- **Predetermined elements**: treat as constraints, not axes
- **Critical uncertainties**: candidates for scenario axes

### Phase 3: Select the axes

Choose 2 critical uncertainties that are:
1. High impact on the focal question
2. Genuinely uncertain (neither outcome is clearly more likely)
3. Relatively independent of each other

Name each axis as a dimension with two poles:
- Example axis 1: "AI trip planning adoption" → *Niche* vs *Dominant (AI mediates >60% of discovery)*
- Example axis 2: "Regulatory environment" → *Open (light touch)* vs *Fragmented (national data sovereignty rules)*

### Phase 4: Name and populate 4 scenarios

For each scenario:
- **World description** (3–5 sentences): what does this world look, feel, and operate like?
- **Who wins**: which businesses, models, or strategies thrive?
- **Who loses**: who gets disrupted or made irrelevant?
- **Key dynamics**: what are the dominant competitive forces?

### Phase 5: Strategic implications per scenario

- What is the optimal strategy in this world?
- What capabilities are critical?
- What investments would be wasted?

Identify: **robust strategies** (perform reasonably in all 4 scenarios) vs **scenario-specific bets**.

**Rule**: lean toward robust strategies unless you have strong evidence for which scenario is most likely.

### Phase 6: Early indicators

For each scenario, name 2–3 observable indicators that would confirm the world is moving toward that quadrant.

### Output format

```
FOCAL QUESTION: [one sentence]

AXES:
  Axis 1: [name] — [Pole A] ←→ [Pole B]
  Axis 2: [name] — [Pole A] ←→ [Pole B]

SCENARIOS:
  [Name] (Axis1-PoleX + Axis2-PoleY)
  World: [3–5 sentence description]
  Winners: [list]
  Losers: [list]
  Key dynamics: [2–3 bullets]

  [repeat for all 4]

ROBUST STRATEGIES (work across scenarios):
  1. ...

SCENARIO-SPECIFIC BETS (name the scenario each requires):
  1. ...

TRIGGER SIGNALS:
  → [Scenario name]: [indicator 1], [indicator 2]
```
