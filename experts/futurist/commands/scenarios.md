# /scenarios — Scenario Planning

**Purpose**: Build a structured 2×2 scenario matrix for a high-uncertainty strategic question. Generate 4 named, vivid, internally consistent future worlds and derive differentiated strategic implications from each.

**When to use**: Strategic planning under high uncertainty; capital allocation decisions; pre-wiring leadership to recognise futures as they emerge.

**Method base**: Shell scenario planning, adapted for company-level strategy.

---

## Phase 1: Define the focal question

State the strategic question the scenarios will inform. Be specific:
- ❌ "What does the future of travel look like?"
- ✅ "How should we allocate product investment between AI-native discovery and traditional search, given uncertainty about how AI reshapes OTA distribution over the next 5 years?"

---

## Phase 2: Identify driving forces

Scan across: **Technology / Economics / Social behaviour / Regulation / Geopolitics / Environment**

Generate 10–15 forces that could materially affect the focal question. For each, rate:
- **Impact** on the focal question: High / Medium / Low
- **Certainty**: Predetermined (will happen regardless) vs Uncertain (could go either way)

Separate:
- **Predetermined elements** (demographic waves, physical laws, already-committed investments): treat as constraints, not axes
- **Critical uncertainties** (genuinely could go either way, high impact): candidates for scenario axes

---

## Phase 3: Select the axes

Choose 2 critical uncertainties that are:
1. High impact on the focal question
2. Genuinely uncertain (neither outcome is clearly more likely)
3. Relatively independent of each other (not just two sides of the same coin)

Name each axis as a dimension with two poles:
- Example axis 1: "AI trip planning adoption" → *Niche (AI as one tool among many)* vs *Dominant (AI mediates >60% of discovery)*
- Example axis 2: "Regulatory environment" → *Open (light touch)* vs *Fragmented (national data sovereignty rules)*

---

## Phase 4: Name and populate 4 scenarios

Place each scenario in a quadrant. Name each scenario evocatively — the name should convey the *feel* of that world, not just describe the axes.

For each scenario:
- **World description** (3–5 sentences): what does this world look, feel, and operate like? What happened to get here?
- **Who wins**: which businesses, models, or strategies thrive?
- **Who loses**: who gets disrupted or made irrelevant?
- **Key dynamics**: what are the dominant competitive forces?

---

## Phase 5: Strategic implications per scenario

For each scenario:
- What is the optimal strategy in this world?
- What capabilities are critical?
- What investments would be wasted?

Identify: **robust strategies** (perform reasonably in all 4 scenarios) vs **scenario-specific bets** (perform brilliantly in one, poorly in others).

**Rule**: lean toward robust strategies unless you have strong evidence for which scenario is most likely.

---

## Phase 6: Early indicators (trigger signals)

For each scenario, name 2–3 observable indicators that would confirm the world is moving toward that quadrant:
- Leading indicators (precede the scenario)
- Lagging indicators (confirm it has arrived)

Build a monitoring list from the high-value indicators across all 4 scenarios.

---

## Output format

```
FOCAL QUESTION: [one sentence]

AXES:
  Axis 1: [name] — [Pole A] ←→ [Pole B]
  Axis 2: [name] — [Pole A] ←→ [Pole B]

SCENARIOS:
  [Name] (Axis1-PoleX + Axis2-PoleY)
  World: [3–5 sentence description]
  Winners: [list]
  Losers: [list]
  Key dynamics: [2–3 bullets]
  
  [repeat for all 4]

ROBUST STRATEGIES (work across scenarios):
  1. ...

SCENARIO-SPECIFIC BETS (name the scenario each requires):
  1. ...

TRIGGER SIGNALS:
  → [Scenario name]: [indicator 1], [indicator 2]
```
