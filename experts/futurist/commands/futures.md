# /futures — Full Futures Analysis

**Purpose**: Comprehensive futures analysis for a topic, sector, or strategic question. Covers structural forces, emerging signals, scenario space, and near-term actions. The broad-scan version of /scenarios.

**When to use**: When you need the full picture, not just scenarios. Use before /scenarios to do the initial scan; use standalone when the goal is exploration rather than scenario planning.

---

## STEEPV framework scan

Run each lens systematically. Goal: surface structural forces, not just trends.

### S — Social & behavioural
- Demographic shifts (ageing, urbanisation, household formation, workforce composition)
- Value shifts (what the next consumer cohort prioritises vs the current one)
- Behavioural changes enabled by technology (what becomes effortless that was effortful)
- Trust dynamics (who do people trust, and how is that shifting?)

### T — Technology
- Where are the major capability S-curves? (AI, biotech, energy, materials, space)
- What is moving from experiment to deployment in the 2–5 year window?
- What cost curves are crossing thresholds? (compute, genome sequencing, solar, battery storage)
- What convergences are appearing? (AI × biology, AI × robotics, energy × mobility)

### E — Economic
- Productivity wave: are we in the installation or deployment phase of the current paradigm?
- Debt cycle position: where are major economies in the long-term debt cycle?
- Capital flows: what sectors are capital-attracting vs capital-fleeing?
- Geopolitical economics: supply chain reshoring, sanctions regimes, digital payments fragmentation

### E — Environmental & physical
- Climate: physical risk timeline; transition risk timeline (policy, stranded assets, insurance)
- Energy: cost of clean energy generation vs incumbents; timeline to parity by geography
- Resource constraints: water, rare earth minerals, arable land — where do these bite?
- Physical infrastructure: what is being built at scale that changes geography? (ports, data centres, power grids)

### P — Political & regulatory
- Regulatory waves: what areas are moving from permissive to restrictive? (AI, data, platform competition, biotech)
- Geopolitical realignment: US-China decoupling, EU as regulatory superpower, MENA strategic reorientation
- Democratic stability: where is political risk elevated and how does it affect economic activity?

### V — Values & worldview
- What does the rising generation believe that the current leadership cohort does not?
- How are concepts of work, ownership, privacy, and trust evolving?
- What institutional legitimacy is eroding? (media, government, corporations, scientific consensus)

---

## Structural forces synthesis

From the STEEPV scan, identify the 3–5 forces that are:
- Structural (not cyclical)
- High impact on the focal domain
- Either predetermined (locked in) or critically uncertain (genuinely either/or)

---

## Scenario space

Based on the structural forces, describe the range of possible futures:
- What is the most probable trajectory (if current forces continue)?
- What is the most disruptive trajectory (if the biggest uncertainty resolves adversely)?
- What is the most optimistic trajectory (if the biggest uncertainty resolves favourably)?

---

## Strategic implications

For each of the three trajectories:
- What is the optimal strategic response?
- What capabilities are required?
- What investments would be misallocated?

---

## Output: standard six-element structure

1. **Structural shift**: what is changing at a deep level
2. **Second-order effects**: what changes as a result that most haven't noticed
3. **Strategic implications**: what this means for the organisation
4. **Breakpoints**: what would change this view substantially
5. **12–24 month actions**: what to do now
6. **Signals to monitor**: early indicators of which trajectory is materialising
