# /provocation — Strategic Provocations

**Purpose**: Generate structured provocations to challenge conventional thinking in a domain. These are not predictions — they are thought experiments designed to expose the brittleness of current assumptions and open up the strategic imagination.

**When to use**: Strategy workshops, annual planning, when thinking has become too consensus-driven, when the team needs to think outside the current planning horizon.

---

## What a good provocation does

A provocation:
- **Challenges a hidden assumption** that most people treat as a fact
- **Is specific enough to be uncomfortable** — vague provocations produce vague thinking
- **Is plausible enough to be taken seriously** — it doesn't have to be likely, but it can't be obviously absurd
- **Generates strategy-relevant implications** — the point is not to be contrarian but to expose strategic optionality

A provocation is not a prediction. When asked "do you really think this will happen?" the answer is: "I don't know. Does your strategy still work if it does?"

---

## The six provocation types

### 1. The Reversal
Take the dominant assumption and reverse it.

*Template*: "What if [the thing everyone believes is increasing] actually [reverses / plateaus / collapses]?"

*Examples*:
- "What if AI capability improvement plateaus in 3 years — what does your product strategy look like then?"
- "What if remote work reverses and 90% of workers return to offices — what does that do to business travel economics?"
- "What if China stops manufacturing cheaply and becomes a premium consumer market — who wins in travel?"

### 2. The Extreme
Take a trend and project it to its logical extreme.

*Template*: "What if [current trend] continues to its maximum logical conclusion?"

*Examples*:
- "What if every trip was booked by an AI agent with no human browsing a single OTA page — what does the OTA business model look like?"
- "What if every knowledge worker's productivity 10×s in 5 years — what do companies do with the surplus?"
- "What if solar energy becomes functionally free in 15 years — what does that do to geopolitics, manufacturing, and travel patterns?"

### 3. The Inversion
Identify who benefits from the current model and ask: who wins if the model breaks?

*Template*: "The current model benefits [X]. If the model broke, who would gain, and what would they do to break it?"

*Examples*:
- "Airlines benefit from OTA distribution costs. If airlines got distribution costs to zero, what does that do to OTA take rates?"
- "Google benefits from OTA bidding wars for search placement. What if Google just became an OTA?"

### 4. The Convergence
Combine two independent trends whose intersection most people haven't considered.

*Template*: "What happens when [trend A] meets [trend B]?"

*Examples*:
- "What happens when aging populations (more retirees with time) meet AI travel planning (frictionless discovery)? Who wins in travel?"
- "What happens when biotech longevity extends working life to 80 × remote work enables digital nomad retirement careers? What does that do to travel demand?"

### 5. The Newcomer
Describe a plausible new entrant that doesn't yet exist but could, and would be structurally superior.

*Template*: "Describe a company founded in [horizon year] that disrupts [current leader] by doing [structural difference]. Why would it be hard to defend against?"

### 6. The Regulation Shock
Describe a plausible regulatory change that would fundamentally reshape the sector.

*Template*: "What if the EU / US / China decided to [specific regulation] — what happens in 24 months?"

---

## Output format

Generate 5–8 provocations. For each:

```
DOMAIN: [sector / question]

PROVOCATIONS:

  [Type: Reversal / Extreme / Inversion / Convergence / Newcomer / Regulation Shock]
  PROVOCATION: "[the specific, uncomfortable statement]"
  Hidden assumption challenged: [what belief does this challenge]
  If true: [what would change — specifically for this company]
  Strategy question it raises: [the strategic question this forces]

  [repeat × 5–8]

MOST UNCOMFORTABLE (the one that gets defensive reactions — explore it):
  [name it] — [why it's the most structurally threatening]
```
