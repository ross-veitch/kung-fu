# /onepager — Executive Memo

**Purpose**: Produce a CEO/board-ready memo on any strategic topic. Ruthlessly compressed. Decision-oriented.

**When to use**: Board prep, investor updates, internal alignment docs, decision memos for senior stakeholders.

**Format**: Pyramid structure (Minto). Conclusion first. Evidence second. Ask last.

---

## Structure

### 1. SITUATION (2–3 sentences)
What is true today. Established, shared context. Not where the narrative lives — just the agreed starting point.

### 2. COMPLICATION (2–3 sentences)
What has changed, or what tension exists. This is the "but" or "however." It creates the reason the memo needs to exist.

### 3. QUESTION (1 sentence)
The specific question this memo answers. Makes it explicit. Orients the reader.

### 4. RECOMMENDATION (1–3 sentences)
The answer to the question. Stated directly. No hedging.

### 5. RATIONALE (3–5 bullets)
Why this recommendation. Each bullet: one reason, stated as a fact or finding, not a hope.

### 6. RISKS (2–3 bullets)
What could go wrong. What we're doing about each.

### 7. ASK (1–2 sentences)
What you need from this audience. Specific. Actionable. Dated if possible.

---

## Length target

400–600 words. If it needs to be longer, the thinking isn't done.

---

## Quality checks

- Does the COMPLICATION create genuine tension that makes the RECOMMENDATION necessary?
- Does every sentence in RATIONALE actually support the RECOMMENDATION (not something adjacent)?
- Is the ASK specific enough that the reader knows exactly what "yes" looks like?
- Could a new reader understand the full argument in 90 seconds?
- Does it read like it was written by a human who has a view — not by a committee that hedged everything?

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- ❌ Starting with background that the reader already knows
- ❌ Burying the recommendation in paragraph four
- ❌ Listing risks without mitigations
- ❌ An ASK that says "please review and provide feedback" (not an ask — a cop-out)
- ❌ Using "we believe" or "it appears" where "the data shows" or "this means" is accurate
