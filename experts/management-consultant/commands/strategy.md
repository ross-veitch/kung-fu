# /strategy — Where to Play / How to Win / Moat Plan

**Purpose**: Produce a strategy brief that makes the core choices explicit: arena, winning condition, required capabilities, moat mechanism, and resource requirements.

**When to use**: Annual planning, pivot decisions, market entry, competitive repositioning.

**Framework base**: Roger Martin's Where to Play / How to Win, layered with Hamilton Helmer's 7 Powers for moat identification.

---

## Phase 1: Where to play

Define the arena with specificity. Vague arena definitions produce vague strategies.

Dimensions:
- **Geographies**: which markets, in what order
- **Customer segments**: who exactly (not "SMBs" — which SMBs, at what stage, with what jobs-to-be-done)
- **Product categories**: what problems are in scope vs deliberately out of scope
- **Channels**: how do we reach and serve customers
- **Stages of value chain**: where do we operate vs partner vs avoid

Explicit **not here** choices are as important as **here** choices. A strategy with no exclusions isn't a strategy.

---

## Phase 2: How to win

State the winning condition: why will customers choose us over alternatives, sustainably?

Test against the 7 Powers (Helmer):
1. **Scale economies** — does our cost structure improve as we grow, in a way competitors can't replicate?
2. **Network effects** — does each new user/supplier make the product better for others?
3. **Switching costs** — once customers use us, how painful is it to leave?
4. **Counter-positioning** — does our model require incumbents to cannibalize themselves to compete?
5. **Branding** — do customers pay a premium because of what we represent, beyond functional features?
6. **Cornered resource** — do we have exclusive access to something valuable (data, talent, IP, relationships)?
7. **Process power** — do we have an embedded process advantage that takes years to replicate?

Identify which power(s) this strategy builds toward. If none, name what the plan is to acquire one.

---

## Phase 3: Required capabilities

What must be true about our organisation for this strategy to work?

List 4–6 critical capabilities:
- Which do we have today at sufficient level?
- Which are gap areas (have the function, not the depth)?
- Which are missing entirely (must build or buy)?

For each gap: build / hire / acquire / partner?

---

## Phase 4: Competitive dynamics

Who are we competing against, directly and indirectly?
- What is their response when we execute this strategy?
- Where are we differentiated vs them? Credibly differentiated, not aspirationally?
- What is the risk of their counter-move? How do we pre-empt or absorb it?

---

## Phase 5: Resource requirements

What does executing this strategy actually cost?
- Capital: how much, for what, over what horizon?
- Headcount: key hires / team builds needed
- Time: what's the 12-month checkpoint; what does success look like then?

Flag: what would have to go right for this to work, and what is the single largest execution risk?

---

## Phase 6: Moat plan

Strategies that don't build moats create temporary advantage at best.

State explicitly:
- What power does this strategy build toward?
- What is the earliest signal that the moat is forming (leading indicator)?
- What would stop the moat from forming, and what's the contingency?

---

## Output format

```
STRATEGY BRIEF: [company / product / initiative]
HORIZON: [12/24/36 months]

WHERE TO PLAY:
  Arenas in: [specific description]
  Arenas out: [explicit exclusions]

HOW TO WIN:
  Core winning condition: [1–2 sentences]
  Power being built: [7 Powers label] — [why this is achievable]

REQUIRED CAPABILITIES:
  Have: [list]
  Gap (build/hire): [list]
  Missing (acquire/partner): [list]

COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS:
  Key competitors: [list]
  Our differentiation: [specific]
  Competitive response risk: [named]

RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS:
  Capital: [range and purpose]
  Key hires: [roles]
  12-month checkpoint: [what success looks like]

MOAT PLAN:
  Target power: [label]
  Leading indicator: [metric/signal]
  Risk to moat formation: [named]

KEY UNCERTAINTIES (rank 1–3):
  1. ...
```
