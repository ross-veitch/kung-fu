# /draft-agreement

Draft a commercial agreement from a deal brief. Produces a working first draft suitable for negotiation — not execution without legal review.

## Steps

1. **Gather the brief**: Who are the parties? What is the commercial relationship? What are the key obligations on each side? What jurisdiction governs?
2. **Identify the agreement type**: SaaS/subscription, services, licence, distribution, partnership, NDA, employment — each has different standard structures.
3. **Note the non-negotiables**: Are there any terms that must be in or out regardless of negotiation?
4. **Draft the key commercial terms first**: The deal-specific parts (scope, fees, term, IP ownership, data handling). Boilerplate follows.
5. **Apply standard positions**: Limitation of liability, indemnification, termination, governing law — apply the positions from `~~contract-playbook` or general commercial standards if no playbook is configured.
6. **Flag open issues**: Identify any terms that will require client instruction or negotiation.

## Output format

**Agreement**: [Full draft in standard commercial format]

**Drafting notes**:
- [Key terms flagged for review before sending to counterparty]
- [Open issues requiring instruction]
- [Deviations from standard positions and rationale]

**Disclaimer**: This draft is for negotiation purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Review by a licensed attorney is required before execution.
