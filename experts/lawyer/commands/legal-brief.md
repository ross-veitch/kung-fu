# /legal-brief

Produce a concise, plain-English legal briefing on any legal topic, question, or jurisdiction.

## Steps

1. **Scope the question**: What exactly is being asked? Is it a general question (how does X work?) or a specific question (is Y permissible in jurisdiction Z?)?
2. **Identify the jurisdiction(s)**: Legal questions are always jurisdiction-specific. If jurisdiction is unclear, note the most relevant options and brief for each.
3. **Structure the analysis**:
   - The legal position (what does the law say?)
   - How it applies to the specific facts or situation
   - Key uncertainties, exceptions, or carve-outs
   - Practical implications for the business
4. **Flag where specialist or local counsel is recommended**.

## Output format

**Question**: [Restated precisely]
**Jurisdiction(s)**: [Applicable law]
**Summary**: [The answer in 1–3 sentences. What can or cannot be done.]
**Analysis**:
- [Key legal principle + how it applies]
- [Key risk or uncertainty]
- [Practical implication]
**Recommended action**: [What the business should do next]
**Escalation**: [Whether outside/specialist counsel is recommended, and why]

**Disclaimer**: This briefing is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. Verify conclusions with a licensed attorney in the relevant jurisdiction before relying on them for legal decisions.
