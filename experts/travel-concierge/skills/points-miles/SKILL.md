# Points & Miles Strategy

## The fundamental principle

Points and miles are a currency — and like any currency, they have an exchange rate. The goal is always: **earn at a high rate, redeem at a high rate**. Most people do one or the other. The best travel hackers do both.

Average redemption value (approximate CPP — cents per point):
| Programme | Typical cash value | Sweet spot redemption |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer | 1.5–2.5¢ | Saver awards, partner airlines in J/F |
| Emirates Skywards | 1.5–2.0¢ | Emirates J/F on own metal |
| Marriott Bonvoy | 0.7–0.9¢ | Category 1–4 hotels, PointSavers |
| Hyatt World of Hyatt | 1.5–2.0¢ | Category 6–7 properties, hotels with high cash rates |
| IHG One Rewards | 0.5–0.7¢ | PointBreaks sales, points + cash |
| Hilton Honors | 0.4–0.6¢ | 5th night free on award stays |

Rule of thumb: if you can't redeem at > 1¢/point, consider holding or transferring.

## Airline alliance architecture

Understanding alliances is prerequisite knowledge for routing optimisation:

**Star Alliance**: United, Singapore Airlines, Lufthansa, ANA, Air Canada, Turkish Airlines, THAI + 21 others. Key for Asia-Pacific routing.

**Oneworld**: British Airways, Cathay Pacific, Qatar Airways, American, Finnair, Qantas, Malaysia Airlines + 9 others. Strong for Middle East and South Asia.

**SkyTeam**: Delta, Air France/KLM, Korean Air, China Southern + 14 others. Weaker in premium Asia routing.

Key insight: **Alliance membership enables award booking on partner airlines**. Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer miles can be used on Lufthansa, ANA, United — often at better rates than those airlines' own programmes.

## Transfer partner networks

Credit card points are the most flexible currency because they can transfer to multiple airline and hotel programmes:

**American Express Membership Rewards** transfers to: Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer, Emirates Skywards, Cathay Pacific Asia Miles, British Airways Avios, Air France/KLM Flying Blue, Hilton, Marriott, and more. Transfer ratios typically 1:1 or 1:0.75.

**Chase Ultimate Rewards** (US-focused but relevant): United, Hyatt, British Airways, Air France/KLM, Singapore Airlines, IHG.

Key rule: **Never redeem credit card points at face value for cash or statement credits** (typically 1¢/point). Transfer them to travel programmes and redeem for 3–5× more value.

## Earning strategy

### Airlines
- Status matters more than points: status unlocks upgrades, lounge access, priority boarding — the "soft benefits" that make travel comfortable
- Earn on credit card spend, not just flying. The best airline miles earners fly far less than they spend.
- Prioritise programmes where you already have (or can achieve) status

### Hotels
- Elite status tiers: typically Silver/Gold/Platinum/Titanium equivalent
- Elite benefits that matter most: late checkout, room upgrades, lounge access, guaranteed elite benefits
- **Prioritise one or two chains** to concentrate status rather than spreading across six programmes with no status anywhere

## Sweet spots (high-value redemptions)

**KrisFlyer**: Singapore – London / Singapore – Tokyo in Business Class (Saver: 85,000–90,000 miles one-way). Partner redemptions on Lufthansa and ANA in First Class can be exceptional value.

**Hyatt**: Park Hyatt properties during shoulder season. Category 7 hotels that would otherwise cost $500+/night at ~25,000 points/night.

**Bonvoy**: Points Transfer to airlines (30,000 Bonvoy = 10,000 airline miles + 5,000 bonus miles at 60K threshold). Also: Category 4–5 redemptions at $200–300/night properties.

**Avios (British Airways)**: Short-haul redemptions on Iberia (Europe) and American (US domestic) at very low mile rates. Also excellent for Gulf destinations on Qatar.

## The award booking process

1. Identify the route and preferred airlines
2. Check saver award availability (most programmes have two tiers: saver/standard; always target saver)
3. Verify you have enough points, or identify transfer partners to top up
4. Check for fuel surcharges — some programmes (especially BA Avios) charge heavy surcharges; others (KrisFlyer, Hyatt) do not
5. Book as early as possible (330 days out for most programmes) — saver space disappears
