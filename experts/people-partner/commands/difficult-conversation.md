# /difficult-conversation

Prepare for a difficult management conversation. This includes: underperformance feedback, termination, restructure/role elimination, culture/behavior issues, or compensation disappointment. The output provides recommended approach, specific talking points, likely reactions and how to handle them, documentation requirements, and legal flags.

---

## Step 1: Identify the situation type

**What conversation are we preparing for?**

1. **Underperformance feedback** (not yet termination)
2. **Termination** (for cause: performance, behavior, or restructure/layoff)
3. **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** kickoff
4. **Restructure / role elimination** (not performance-related)
5. **Culture/behavior issue** (e.g., toxic behavior, harassment allegation, ethics violation)
6. **Compensation disappointment** (employee expected raise/promotion, didn't get it)
7. **Demotion or role change** (scope reduction)

## Step 2: Prepare context and evidence

**Required:**
- **Employee name, role, tenure**
- **Situation summary** (what happened? why is this conversation needed?)
- **Evidence** (performance data, examples, documentation)
- **Decision** (what outcome are you seeking? feedback only? PIP? termination?)

**Optional but improves prep:**
- **Past feedback given** (is this a surprise or have you addressed this before?)
- **Manager-employee relationship** (good? strained? broken?)
- **Legal/HR consultation** (have you flagged this to HR? do you need legal review?)

## Situation 1: Underperformance Feedback (Not Yet Termination)

### Goal
Give clear, actionable feedback that the employee can act on. Preserve dignity. Document the conversation.

### Approach

**1. Be direct (first 30 seconds)**
> "I want to talk about your performance. There are some gaps I need to address."

**2. State specific examples (not generalities)**

❌ Bad: "You're not meeting expectations."  
✅ Good: "You missed 4 out of 6 project deadlines this quarter. The booking flow feature was delayed by 3 weeks, which pushed our product launch."

**3. Explain impact**
> "When deadlines are missed, it affects the entire team's ability to ship on time. The product team had to re-plan the roadmap, and we lost a key sales opportunity."

**4. Invite their perspective (but don't let them deflect)**
> "Help me understand what's happening. What's getting in the way?"

**Common deflections + how to handle:**
- "I didn't know this was a priority" → "We discussed this in our 1:1 on [date]. I need you to flag earlier if priorities are unclear."
- "I didn't have enough resources" → "What resources did you need that you didn't ask for?"
- "The requirements kept changing" → "I understand there was one change, but the core deadline didn't move."

**5. Set clear expectations for next period**
> "Here's what I need to see improve: (1) Hit 100% of deadlines for the next 8 weeks. (2) Communicate blockers within 24 hours, not after deadlines are missed. (3) Proactively ask for help if you're behind."

**6. State consequences (if this is serious)**
> "If these gaps don't close in the next [30/60/90 days], we'll need to make a change. I want you to succeed, but improvement is on you."

---

### Likely reactions + how to handle

| Reaction | How to respond |
|---|---|
| **Defensive / blames others** | "I hear you, but I need you to focus on what you can control. What will you do differently?" |
| **Tears / emotional** | Pause. Offer tissue. "I know this is hard to hear. Take a minute." Then continue. |
| **Argues / disagrees** | "I understand you see it differently. This is my assessment based on [data]. Let's focus on what you can do to improve." |
| **Shuts down / silent** | "I need to hear from you. How are you processing this?" (If still silent: "Let's take a break and reconvene tomorrow.") |
| **Accepts responsibility** | "I appreciate you owning this. Let's make a plan to get you back on track." |

---

### Documentation

**What to document (same day)**:
- Date of conversation
- Performance gaps discussed (specific examples)
- Expectations set for improvement
- Employee's response (did they acknowledge? disagree? commit to improve?)
- Next steps (follow-up meeting date, check-in cadence)

**Where to store**: HRIS (BambooHR, Workday) or secure file (if no HRIS).

## Situation 2: Termination (For Cause: Performance or Behavior)

### Goal
End employment clearly, quickly, and legally. Minimize risk. Preserve dignity as much as possible.

### Pre-conversation checklist

- [ ] **HR/Legal review** (required for all terminations)
- [ ] **Documentation ready** (performance reviews, PIP, written warnings, examples)
- [ ] **Severance decision** (if any — check employment contract + local law)
- [ ] **Final paycheck ready** (calculate last paycheck + accrued PTO + severance)
- [ ] **IT offboarding plan** (laptop/badge collected same day)
- [ ] **Internal announcement drafted** (to be sent same day)
- [ ] **Bring HR to the conversation** (witness + support)

---

### Termination conversation structure (10-15 minutes max)

**Setting**: Private room. Have tissues available. HR present.

**1. Be direct (first sentence)**
> "I've made the decision to end your employment, effective [today / end of week / date]."

**2. State reason (1-2 sentences, factual)**
> "Despite the PIP and feedback, the performance gaps have not closed. [OR] We're eliminating your role as part of a restructure."

**3. Logistics (what happens next)**
> "Your last day is [date]. You'll receive [severance details: e.g., 2 weeks pay, continuation of benefits for 30 days]. HR will walk you through the offboarding process and final paycheck details."

**4. Collect company property**
> "I need your laptop and badge before you leave today. [If remote:] We'll send a prepaid box for you to return your laptop."

**5. End the conversation**
> "HR will stay with you to answer any questions about logistics. Thank you for your time here."

---

### What NOT to say

❌ "This was a really hard decision for me" (makes it about you)  
❌ "You're a great person, but..." (mixed messages)  
❌ "I fought for you, but leadership decided..." (throws others under the bus)  
❌ "Maybe we can work together in the future" (false hope)  
❌ "Do you have any questions about why?" (opens debate — the decision is final)

---

### Likely reactions + how to handle

| Reaction | How to respond |
|---|---|
| **Shock / tears** | Pause. Let them process. "I know this is difficult." Don't rush. |
| **Anger / yelling** | Stay calm. "I understand you're upset. The decision is final." If escalates: "I'm going to step out. HR will continue from here." |
| **Bargaining** ("Can I have more time?") | "The decision is final. I'm sorry." |
| **Threats ("I'll sue")** | "You have every right to consult a lawyer. HR will provide you with the termination letter outlining everything." (Alert legal immediately after.) |
| **Accepts calmly** | "Thank you for understanding. HR will take it from here." |

---

### Post-termination actions (same day)

1. **Offboard IT immediately** (revoke access to email, Slack, systems)
2. **Collect laptop/badge** (or arrange return if remote)
3. **Internal announcement** (same day, brief):
   > "[Name] is no longer with the company as of [date]. [Manager] will handle [their work] going forward. Please reach out to [manager/HR] if you have questions."
4. **Document the conversation** (date, what was said, how they reacted, HR present)
5. **Brief the team** (manager should address team in person or via call — don't let rumors fill the vacuum)

## Situation 3: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Kickoff

### Goal
Give the employee a clear, structured path to improve. Document expectations and timeline. (Note: 80-90% of PIPs end in termination, so also treat this as pre-termination documentation.)

### PIP structure (written document)

```markdown
# Performance Improvement Plan: [Employee Name]

**Date**: [Start Date]  
**Duration**: [30/60/90 days]  
**Manager**: [Name]  
**HR Contact**: [Name]

---

## Performance Gaps

[Specific, observable gaps — not vague]

1. **Gap 1**: [Example: Missed 5 out of 7 deadlines in Q1]
2. **Gap 2**: [Example: Code quality required significant rework (3 out of 4 PRs)]
3. **Gap 3**: [Example: Did not incorporate feedback from 1:1s (see notes from Jan 15, Feb 3)]

---

## Required Improvements

[Specific, measurable outcomes]

1. **Improvement 1**: Hit 100% of agreed deadlines for the next 60 days. Any at-risk deadlines must be flagged 48 hours in advance with a mitigation plan.
2. **Improvement 2**: Code must pass code review on first submission (no major rework required) for 80% of PRs.
3. **Improvement 3**: Demonstrate incorporation of feedback by implementing suggestions from 1:1s within 1 week of discussion.

---

## Support Provided

[What the company/manager will do to help]

- Weekly 1:1s with manager (30 minutes)
- Pairing sessions with [Senior Engineer Name] (2 hours/week)
- Access to [training resource / course]

---

## Check-ins

- **Weekly**: Progress check (every Friday, 30 min)
- **Mid-point review** (Day 30): Assess progress; adjust plan if needed
- **Final review** (Day 60): Determine outcome (pass/fail)

---

## Success Criteria

By [End Date], you must demonstrate:
- 100% on-time delivery (or flagged early with mitigation)
- 80% code quality (first-pass approval)
- Positive feedback from manager on responsiveness to feedback

---

## Consequences

If these gaps are not closed by [End Date], your employment will be terminated.

---

**Employee Signature**: _____________________ Date: _____  
**Manager Signature**: _____________________ Date: _____  
**HR Signature**: _____________________ Date: _____
```

---

### PIP kickoff conversation

**1. Be direct**
> "I'm putting you on a Performance Improvement Plan. This is a formal process that gives you a clear path to improve, but I need to be honest: if these gaps don't close in the next 60 days, your employment will end."

**2. Walk through the PIP document**
> "Here are the specific gaps [read them]. Here's what I need to see improve [read requirements]. Here's the support I'm providing [read support]. We'll check in weekly."

**3. Get their signature**
> "I need you to sign this to acknowledge you've received it. Signing doesn't mean you agree, just that you've seen it."

**4. Set first check-in**
> "Our first weekly check-in is [day/time]. Come prepared with an update on [specific deliverable]."

## Situation 4: Restructure / Role Elimination (Not Performance-Related)

### Goal
Deliver the news clearly and with empathy. Explain the business rationale. Provide severance (if applicable).

### Approach

**1. Lead with empathy (but still be direct)**
> "I have difficult news. We're restructuring [team/function], and your role is being eliminated. This is not about your performance — it's a business decision."

**2. Explain the business rationale (briefly)**
> "We're consolidating [X and Y teams] to reduce costs and simplify the org structure. As a result, [number] roles are being eliminated, including yours."

**3. Severance and support**
> "You'll receive [severance package: e.g., 8 weeks pay + benefits through end of quarter]. We're also providing [outplacement support / job search assistance / reference letter]."

**4. Timeline**
> "Your last day is [date]. You can choose to work through [date] or leave earlier with full pay through [date]."

**5. References and goodwill**
> "I'm happy to serve as a reference. You've done strong work here, and I'll make sure that's reflected."

---

### Likely reactions

| Reaction | How to respond |
|---|---|
| "Is this about my performance?" | "No. This is a business decision about the role, not about you." |
| "Can I move to another team?" | "I'll check if there are open roles that fit your skills. HR will share internal postings with you." (Only say this if it's true.) |
| "Why me and not [other person]?" | "I can't discuss other people's situations. This decision was based on business need and role structure." |
| "This is unfair." | "I understand this feels unfair. I'm sorry." (Don't argue.) |

## Situation 5: Culture/Behavior Issue (e.g., Toxic Behavior, Harassment Allegation)

### Goal
Address the behavior immediately and clearly. Protect the company and other employees. Follow legal/HR protocols strictly.

### Pre-conversation (CRITICAL)

- [ ] **Consult HR + Legal immediately** (do not proceed without them)
- [ ] **Investigation complete** (if allegation, must investigate before disciplinary conversation)
- [ ] **Evidence documented** (witness statements, Slack logs, emails, etc.)
- [ ] **Decision made** (warning? suspension? termination?)

---

### Conversation structure (HR must be present)

**1. State the issue (factual, not accusatory)**
> "We need to discuss a serious concern. [Describe behavior: e.g., Multiple team members have reported that you made comments in meetings that were [demeaning / inappropriate / harassment].] "

**2. Share evidence (specific examples)**
> "On [date], you said [quote]. On [date], you did [action]. This violates our [code of conduct / harassment policy]."

**3. Give them a chance to respond**
> "I want to hear your side. What's your perspective?"

**(Listen, but don't let them minimize or deflect.)**

**4. State the consequence**
> - **Warning**: "This is a formal warning. If this behavior happens again, you will be terminated."
> - **Suspension**: "You're suspended with pay for [X days] while we complete the investigation."
> - **Termination**: "This behavior is unacceptable. Your employment is terminated effective immediately."

**5. Document everything**
> HR takes detailed notes. Employee signs acknowledgment (or refusal to sign is documented).

---

## Situation 6: Compensation Disappointment

### Goal
Deliver the news clearly, explain the rationale, and manage expectations without creating false hope.

### Conversation structure

**1. Deliver the news directly**
> "I know you were hoping for [raise/promotion], but the decision is [you're not getting it / you're getting X instead of Y]."

**2. Explain why (specific, not vague)**

✅ Good: "Promotion to Senior Engineer requires [specific criteria]. You're strong in [A and B], but you haven't yet demonstrated [C: e.g., ownership of end-to-end projects]. That's what I need to see in the next 6 months."

❌ Bad: "It's not the right time" / "Budget constraints" (too vague, feels like an excuse)

**3. Give a path forward (if there is one)**
> "Here's what you need to do to earn [promotion/raise] in the next review cycle: [specific, measurable goals]."

**4. Manage expectations**
> "If you hit these goals, I'll advocate for [promotion/raise] in [next cycle]. But I can't guarantee it — it also depends on [business performance / budget / calibration]."

---

### Likely reactions

| Reaction | How to respond |
|---|---|
| "But [peer] got promoted and I'm better than them!" | "I can't discuss other people's comp. Let's focus on your path forward." |
| "I'm going to look for other jobs." | "I'd hate to lose you, but I understand. Let me know if you want to talk through your career path here." (Don't beg or make promises you can't keep.) |
| "This is unfair." | "I hear you. I don't expect you to agree, but this is my assessment." |

## General Best Practices (All Difficult Conversations)

### Before the conversation

1. **Consult HR/Legal** if termination, PIP, behavior issue, or restructure
2. **Document everything** (performance data, examples, past feedback)
3. **Have a clear outcome in mind** (what decision have you made?)
4. **Choose the right setting** (private room, no interruptions, HR present if needed)

### During the conversation

1. **Be direct** (don't bury the lead with small talk)
2. **Be brief** (10-20 minutes max; don't drag it out)
3. **Be factual** (not emotional or personal)
4. **Don't apologize for the decision** (empathy is fine; apologizing undermines your authority)
5. **Listen, but don't debate** (they can share their perspective, but the decision is final)

### After the conversation

1. **Document immediately** (same day, while memory is fresh)
2. **Brief stakeholders** (HR, leadership, team if needed)
3. **Follow through on commitments** (severance, offboarding, references, etc.)

---

## Legal Red Flags (When to Involve Legal Immediately)

- **Discrimination claim** (employee says they're being treated unfairly due to race/gender/age/religion/etc.)
- **Harassment allegation** (sexual harassment, hostile work environment)
- **Retaliation claim** (employee says they're being punished for whistleblowing or reporting misconduct)
- **Threat of lawsuit** (employee mentions "lawyer" or "sue")
- **Large severance** (>3 months pay) or contentious negotiation
- **Employee in protected category** (pregnant, on medical leave, recently filed complaint)

**Action**: Stop. Do not proceed without legal counsel.
