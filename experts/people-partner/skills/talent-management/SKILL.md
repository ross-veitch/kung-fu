# Talent Management

## Performance Management

### Calibration
Managers meet to align ratings across teams before finalising reviews. Eliminates manager bias; forces honest conversations about who is truly high-performing.

**Forced curve**: Top 20% Exceeds / Middle 70% Meets / Bottom 10% Below. Good for large orgs and sales teams; demotivating if applied rigidly to high-talent-density teams.

**No curve**: Manager discretion. Flexible; prone to grade inflation.

**Recommended hybrid**: Calibrate without forcing a curve, but flag outliers ("60% Exceeds on this team — is that defensible?").

### 9-Box Grid (Performance × Potential)

|  | Low Performance | Medium Performance | High Performance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High Potential** | Potential Gem (coach or exit) | High Potential (develop; promote in 12-18mo) | Star / Successor (retain at all costs) |
| **Medium Potential** | Enigma (PIP or exit) | Core Contributor (backbone; recognise) | High Performer (reward; may be topped out) |
| **Low Potential** | Poor Fit (exit fast) | Effective (keep or exit by business need) | Solid Performer (keep in role) |

**Rule**: invest 70% of development resources in High Potential row. Don't over-invest in Low Potential row.

## High-Performer Retention

### Why they leave
1. Compensation not competitive (they get external offers)
2. No growth path (topped out, no promotion in sight)
3. Bad manager (#1 reason for quitting)
4. Bored / unchallenged
5. Mission drift (don't believe in the strategy anymore)

### Retention by cause

| Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| Compensation gap | Off-cycle raise, equity refresh, promotion |
| No growth path | Stretch project, new role, lateral move |
| Bad manager | Move to different team, or coach the manager |
| Bored | Give ownership of strategic initiative |
| Mission drift | Honest conversation — can't be faked |

**Warning signs**: suddenly quiet in meetings (used to be vocal); spikes in PTO; starts asking "what's next for me here?"

**Proactive retention**: quarterly career conversations (not just annual reviews); equity refreshes every 12-18 months for stars; visibility with senior leadership.

## Compensation Design

### Market-rate vs value-based comp
Market rate = what competitors pay (benchmark via Radford, Mercer, Levels.fyi). Value-based = what the role is worth to your business. Pay above market for: critical roles, high performers, high retention risk. Pay at market for: core contributors, easily replaceable roles.

### Comp bands

| Level | Min | Mid | Max |
|---|---|---|---|
| IC3 | $100K | $120K | $140K |
| IC4 | $140K | $165K | $190K |

Band width: typically 40-50% spread. New hires land at 50th-75th percentile (room to grow). Topped-out employees hit band max — must promote for more salary. Merit increases: 3-8%. Promotion increases: 10-20%.

### Equity refreshes
Early employees' equity vests out → retention risk spikes. Refresh cadence: every 12-18 months for high performers. Sizing: Stars 50-70% of original grant, High performers 30-50%, Core 10-30%.

## Multi-Jurisdiction Basics

*Not legal advice. Always involve employment counsel for specific situations.*

**Singapore**: Employment Passes (EP) require minimum salary + COMPASS scoring. CPF: employer contributes 17% for citizens/PRs. Retrenchment: notify MOM; benefits typically 2 weeks-1 month per year of service. No at-will employment.

**UAE (Dubai/Abu Dhabi)**: DIFC (common law, employee-friendly) vs Mainland (local labor law). End-of-service gratuity: 21 days basic salary/year (first 5 years), 30 days/year (6+), capped at 2 years total. Visa tied to employer — employee must find new sponsor or leave within 30 days of termination.

**Saudi Arabia**: Nitaqat quotas (Saudization) — must hire required % of Saudi nationals or face visa/hiring penalties. End-of-service: half-month per year (first 5), one month per year (after 5). Women in workforce growing rapidly post-Vision 2030.

**India**: Strong permanent employee protections; retrenchment of 100+ employees requires government approval. PF: employer + employee each contribute 12% of basic. Gratuity: 15 days per year after 5 years of service. Employment law varies significantly by state.

## PIPs and Terminations

**PIPs in practice**: 80-90% are exit documentation. The 10-20% where they help: role mismatch (move them) or genuine skill gap (trainable, motivated). Don't use for capability mismatch or broken trust — waste of everyone's time.

**PIP structure (if warranted)**: specific observable gaps + measurable outcomes required + support provided + weekly check-ins + clear pass/fail criteria + explicit consequence if failed.

**Termination conversation**: be direct in the first sentence ("I've made the decision to end your employment, effective [date]"). State reason briefly and factually. Cover logistics (last day, severance, offboarding). Don't debate — the decision is final. Keep it to 10-15 minutes. Collect equipment same day; IT offboards immediately; internal announcement same day.
