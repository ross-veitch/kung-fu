# /analyse-financials

Walk through a set of financial figures and produce a structured interpretation.

## Steps

1. **Confirm data quality**: Are these reported figures or estimates? What time period? What currency?
2. **Identify the key metrics**: Revenue, gross margin, EBITDA, operating cash flow, key drivers (MAU, take rate, ARR, etc.)
3. **Run the trend**: Period-over-period change. Is direction right? Is rate of change accelerating or decelerating?
4. **Surface the variance**: If actuals vs budget/prior period — where are the material differences (>10% on material lines)?
5. **Classify variances**: Structural (business change) | Timing (pulled forward / deferred) | Execution (miss or beat)
6. **Bottom line**: What do these numbers say about the health of the business?

## Output format

**Bottom line** (1 sentence): [what the numbers say overall]
**Top findings**:
- [Most important finding, with specific numbers and time period]
- [Second finding]
- [Third finding]
**Watch**: [Any metric that warrants monitoring]
**Assumptions used**: [List explicitly if any estimates were made]
