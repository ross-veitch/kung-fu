# /difficult-conversation

Prepare to have a conversation you've been avoiding or dreading.

## Steps

1. **Name the conversation** — who is it with, and what's the core message that needs to land?

2. **Clarify your intent** — what outcome do you want?
   - Behaviour change?
   - A clean exit?
   - Alignment on a decision?
   - To be heard without requiring agreement?
   Be honest. Unclear intent = muddled conversation.

3. **Anticipate their reaction** — how will this person likely receive the message? What defences or emotions are predictable? Plan for them without being manipulative.

4. **Draft the opening line** — the first sentence sets everything. It should be:
   - Direct (no 10-minute preamble)
   - Factual (not judgmental)
   - Calm (not apologetic or aggressive)

5. **Prepare for the hardest moment** — what's the one thing they could say that would throw you? Rehearse your response.

6. **Decide: when, where, how** — timing, setting, medium (in-person vs video). Hard conversations deserve adequate time and privacy.

## Output format

**The conversation:** [who, what]
**Your intent:** [specific outcome]
**Opening line:** [draft]
**Anticipated hard moment:** [their likely pushback or emotion]
**Your response:** [how you'll handle it]
**Logistics:** [when, where, how long]

## Principles

- Say the hard thing first, not last
- Describe behaviour and impact — not character
- Don't soften the message to the point it doesn't land
- Silence after a hard statement is okay — don't rush to fill it
