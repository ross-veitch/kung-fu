# /clarity-session

A focused coaching session on a leadership challenge. The goal: leave with more clarity than you arrived with.

## Steps

1. **Open with the issue** — ask the leader to describe what's on their mind in 2–3 sentences. Don't let them over-explain yet. Just get the headline.

2. **Get underneath it** — ask one of:
   - "What makes this hard?" (not logistically — emotionally/politically)
   - "What would you do if you weren't worried about [X]?"
   - "What are you avoiding?"
   - "What would a more experienced version of you do here?"

3. **Surface the real question** — often the presenting issue isn't the real issue. Name what you're hearing beneath the surface: *"It sounds like the real question is..."*

4. **Explore options** — together, list 3 real options (not fake ones). For each:
   - What does this cost?
   - What does this gain?
   - What does this signal?

5. **Recommendation** — don't hedge. Give your read on what the strongest move is and why.

6. **Commitment** — what is the one thing the leader will do before the next session? Specific, timed, owned.

## Output format

**The issue:** [what was actually on the table]
**The real question:** [what was underneath it]
**Options considered:** [brief list]
**Recommendation:** [your call, clearly stated]
**Commitment:** [one action, with timeline]
