# /decision-unpacker

For decisions that deserve more than 10 minutes of thought.

## Steps

1. **State the decision clearly** — not the situation, not the background: the specific choice. "I am deciding whether to [X] or [Y]."

2. **Classify the decision**
   - Reversible vs irreversible (two-way vs one-way door)
   - Time-sensitive vs patient
   - Yours to make alone vs requires alignment
   Different types deserve different amounts of process.

3. **What would need to be true?** — for each option, ask: "What would have to be true about the world for this to be the right call?" This separates facts from assumptions.

4. **Pre-mortem both paths** — fast-forward 12 months.
   - If you chose A and it went badly: what happened?
   - If you chose B and it went badly: what happened?
   Which failure is more recoverable? Which are you more afraid of?

5. **Who else has context?** — is there someone with more relevant experience you should talk to before deciding? What do they know that you don't?

6. **The 10/10/10 test** — how will you feel about this decision in 10 minutes? 10 months? 10 years?

7. **Make the call** — after this process, what's your decision? State it clearly. Ambiguity after structured reflection usually means fear, not genuine uncertainty.

## Output format

**The decision:** [specific choice between specific options]
**Classification:** [reversibility / urgency / who decides]
**Key assumption per option:** [what would need to be true]
**Pre-mortem worst case:** [Option A failure / Option B failure]
**Your call:** [the decision, stated clearly]
**Next action:** [what you do in the next 24 hours to execute it]
