# /pitch-book

Structure a management presentation or investor pitch. Output includes the narrative arc, slide-by-slide outline, key messages per section, and specific data points that should anchor each slide.

---

## Step 1: Identify pitch type

| Pitch Type | Audience | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **M&A Sell-Side** | Potential acquirers | Maximize valuation, create competitive tension |
| **Fundraise (Series A+)** | VCs, growth equity | Secure funding at target valuation |
| **Board Update / Strategic Review** | Board of directors | Get approval for strategic decision |
| **IC Deck (Investment Committee)** | Internal investment team (PE/VC) | Get approval to invest |
| **IPO Roadshow** | Public market investors | Generate demand for IPO |

- ~~audience-profile (strategic buyers, financial buyers, VCs)
- ~~pitch-timeline (how long is the presentation? 15 min? 45 min?)

---

## Step 2: Build the narrative arc (SCR: Situation-Complication-Resolution)

### Situation
**What**: Set the stage. What industry/market are we in? What opportunity exists?

**Slides**: Market overview, market size (TAM/SAM/SOM), trends

### Complication
**What**: What problem exists that creates the opportunity? Why now?

**Slides**: Customer pain points, competitive landscape, why incumbents are failing

### Resolution
**What**: How does this company solve the problem? Why is it winning?

**Slides**: Product/solution, business model, traction, competitive advantages

---

## Step 3: Select slide structure by pitch type

### M&A Sell-Side Deck (Management Presentation)

**Goal**: Position company as strategic asset, demonstrate growth potential, create urgency.

**Slide count**: 25-35 slides (45-60 min presentation)

**Slide outline**:

1. **Cover**: Company name, "Confidential — For Discussion Purposes Only," Date
2. **Executive Summary** (1 slide): Investment highlights (5-7 bullets)
3. **Company Overview** (2-3 slides): What we do, history, geography, customer base
4. **Market Opportunity** (3-4 slides): TAM/SAM/SOM, market trends, growth drivers
5. **Product/Technology** (3-5 slides): Product overview, differentiation, roadmap
6. **Business Model** (2-3 slides): Revenue model, pricing, unit economics
7. **Competitive Positioning** (2-3 slides): Competitive landscape, why we win
8. **Customers & Case Studies** (2-3 slides): Key customers, testimonials, use cases
9. **Growth & Traction** (4-5 slides): Revenue growth, user growth, cohort retention, pipeline
10. **Go-To-Market** (2-3 slides): Sales/marketing strategy, CAC/LTV, channel mix
11. **Operations** (2-3 slides): Team (org chart, key hires), tech stack, scalability
12. **Financials** (4-5 slides): Historical P&L, projections (3-5 years), unit economics, cash flow
13. **Strategic Rationale** (2-3 slides): Why acquire us? Synergies, market positioning
14. **Appendix**: Detailed financials, customer list, org chart, product screenshots

---

### Fundraise Deck (Series A / Growth Equity)

**Goal**: Show traction, demonstrate scalability, articulate path to $100M+ revenue.

**Slide count**: 15-20 slides (30-45 min presentation)

**Slide outline**:

1. **Cover**
2. **The Problem** (1 slide): Customer pain point
3. **The Solution** (1 slide): How we solve it
4. **Market Opportunity** (1-2 slides): TAM/SAM/SOM
5. **Product** (2-3 slides): Product demo, key features
6. **Traction** (2-3 slides): Revenue, users, growth rate, key metrics
7. **Business Model** (1 slide): How we make money
8. **Go-To-Market** (1 slide): CAC/LTV, sales channels
9. **Competitive Landscape** (1 slide): Why we win
10. **Team** (1 slide): Founders, key hires
11. **Financial Projections** (1-2 slides): 3-year forecast
12. **Use of Funds** (1 slide): What we'll do with the money
13. **Ask** (1 slide): Raising $Xm at $Ym valuation

---

### Board Update / Strategic Decision

**Goal**: Get board approval for a strategic decision (M&A, major pivot, capital raise).

**Slide count**: 10-15 slides (20-30 min presentation)

**Slide outline**:

1. **Cover**
2. **Executive Summary** (1 slide): Recommendation + key rationale
3. **Situation** (2-3 slides): What's happening? Why now?
4. **Options Considered** (2-3 slides): Build vs buy vs partner, pros/cons
5. **Recommendation** (1 slide): What we recommend + why
6. **Financial Impact** (2-3 slides): Valuation, IRR, ROI, P&L impact
7. **Risks & Mitigation** (1 slide): Key risks + how we address them
8. **Next Steps** (1 slide): Timeline, approvals needed, what we need from board

---

### IC Deck (Investment Committee — PE/VC)

**Goal**: Get internal approval to invest. Show downside protection, upside potential, clear exit path.

**Slide count**: 20-30 slides (45-60 min presentation)

**Slide outline**:

1. **Investment Thesis** (1 slide): Why we should invest (3-5 bullets)
2. **Company Overview** (2-3 slides): Business model, market position
3. **Market Opportunity** (2-3 slides): TAM, trends, competitive dynamics
4. **Management Team** (1-2 slides): Track record, capability assessment
5. **Financials & Projections** (4-5 slides): Historical + forecast, unit economics
6. **Valuation** (2-3 slides): DCF, comps, transaction precedents
7. **Deal Structure** (1-2 slides): Investment amount, ownership %, terms
8. **Investment Highlights** (1 slide): Why this is attractive (growth, margins, defensibility)
9. **Key Risks** (2-3 slides): Market, execution, competition, exit risk
10. **Exit Scenarios** (2-3 slides): IPO, strategic sale, secondary — IRR analysis
11. **Recommendation** (1 slide): Invest / Pass / More DD needed

---

## Step 4: Write slide-level key messages

For each slide, define the **one key takeaway** (what should the audience remember?).

**Example (M&A Sell-Side, Slide: Market Opportunity)**:

**Key Message**: "We operate in a $50B market growing at 15% annually, driven by shift to online booking — and we're capturing share from incumbents."

**Supporting Data**:
- TAM: $50B (source: XYZ Research)
- CAGR: 15% (2024-2028)
- Our market share: 3% today → 8% by 2027 (achievable based on growth trajectory)
- Key trends: Mobile-first users (+25% YoY), millennials prefer online booking (70%), incumbents losing share (-2% YoY)

---

## Step 5: Design data anchors (numbers that stick)

Every slide needs 1-2 **anchor numbers** — the data points that make the slide credible and memorable.

**Example anchors**:

- **Traction slide**: "Revenue grew from $5M → $25M in 18 months (5× growth)"
- **Retention slide**: "90-day retention is 65% (top quartile for the industry)"
- **Customer slide**: "Top 10 customers include 3 Fortune 500 companies"
- **Margin slide**: "Gross margin is 75% (vs industry average of 50%)"

**Rule**: If a slide doesn't have a memorable number, it's not adding value. Cut it or strengthen it.

## Design Principles

1. **One idea per slide** (don't cram)
2. **Lead with the headline** (key message at top)
3. **Data visualizations > tables** (charts are faster to read)
4. **Minimal text** (bullets, not paragraphs)
5. **Consistent format** (same fonts, colors, layout)
6. **Appendix for detail** (main deck is the story, appendix is backup)

## Escalation / Edge cases

**If audience is short on time (15 min instead of 45 min)**:  
- Start with Exec Summary
- Jump to Traction + Financials
- Skip product detail, competitive landscape (unless asked)

**If audience is technical (eng/product team)**:  
- Spend more time on product slides (architecture, tech stack, roadmap)
- Less time on financials

**If audience is financial (PE/VC IC)**:  
- Lead with financials + unit economics
- Spend more time on valuation, exit scenarios, downside protection

**If deck is for email (not live presentation)**:  
- Make slides self-explanatory (more text than you'd use in person)
- Include detailed appendix (anticipate questions)
