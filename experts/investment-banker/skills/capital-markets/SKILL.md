# Capital Markets

How companies raise equity and debt capital — the mechanics of primary issuance, cap table management, and the economics of different financing structures.

---

## Equity raises

### Private rounds (venture and growth equity)

**Round structure**: Seed → Series A → Series B → Series C → Growth → Pre-IPO. Each round involves negotiating valuation, ownership, and governance rights. Term sheets are primarily economic (valuation, option pool) and control (protective provisions, information rights, board seats).

**Cap table mechanics**: Pre-money valuation sets post-money once investment amount is known. Option pool shuffle — investors often require expanding the option pool pre-investment, which dilutes founders. Fully diluted shares include: common, all option grants (vested + unvested), warrants, convertible notes/SAFEs. Know every cap table item before quoting ownership.

**SAFEs and convertible notes**: Non-dilutive at issuance; convert to equity at a future priced round. SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) — no interest, no maturity, converts at discount or valuation cap. Convertible note — accrues interest, has maturity date. Valuation cap determines maximum price at which instruments convert.

**Key terms to negotiate**:
- Liquidation preference (1x non-participating is standard; participating preferred is founder-unfriendly)
- Anti-dilution (broad-based weighted average is market; full ratchet is investor-friendly and founders should resist)
- Information rights, board composition, pro rata rights
- Drag-along and tag-along provisions

### IPO process

**Timeline**: Typically 12–18 months from decision to listing. Key phases: audited financial preparation (3 years), legal restructuring (if needed), bank selection (3–5 banks), S-1/prospectus drafting (10–12 weeks), SEC review (2 rounds, 4–6 weeks each), roadshow (2 weeks), pricing and listing.

**Underwriter role**: Lead left bank manages the book; co-managers support distribution. Gross spread typically 4–7% for a US IPO. Banks build the institutional order book during the roadshow.

**SPAC as alternative**: Special Purpose Acquisition Company. Shell company raises capital via IPO, then merges with target. Faster to market, less regulatory scrutiny on projections, but significant redemptions often leave less cash than anticipated. Market significantly cooled post-2021.

## Debt capital markets

### Leveraged buyout (LBO) financing

**Typical LBO capital structure** (varies by asset type and market conditions):
- Senior secured debt: 3–5x EBITDA — term loans (TLB), revolving credit facility
- Unitranche (direct lending): combines senior + mezzanine, simpler structure
- Mezzanine/subordinated debt: 1–2x EBITDA, higher yield, often PIK (paid-in-kind) option
- Equity contribution: 35–50% of total cap structure

**Key leverage metrics**: Total leverage (Total Debt / EBITDA), Net leverage (Net Debt / EBITDA), interest coverage (EBITDA / Interest Expense > 2x typically required), debt service coverage.

### Investment grade debt

**Credit ratings**: Moody's, S&P, Fitch. Investment grade: BBB-/Baa3 and above. High yield (speculative): BB+/Ba1 and below. Rating determines cost of debt and investor universe.

**Bond mechanics**: Face value (principal), coupon (annual %, paid semi-annually in US), maturity, call provisions (callable bonds allow issuer to repay early). Yield-to-maturity (YTM) = the market price-implied return.

## Dilution analysis

A fundamental skill. Track dilution through every financing event:

```
Ownership % = Shares held / Fully diluted shares outstanding

Post-money valuation = Pre-money + Investment
Post-money price per share = Pre-money / Pre-money shares

Pro forma cap table: show ownership before + after each round, including option pool expansion
```

**Waterfall analysis**: In exit scenarios, model the distribution of proceeds through the liquidation preference stack. Participating preferred holders extract their preference first, then participate pro-rata. Non-participating holders choose: preference or conversion. Model multiple exit scenarios to show different stakeholder outcomes.
