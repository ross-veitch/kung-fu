# MENA Markets

## Saudi Arabia

**Vision 2030**: $500B+ transformation to diversify away from oil
- **Tourism**: Target 100M visitors/year by 2030 (up from 15M in 2019)
- **GIGA projects**: NEOM ($500B mega-city), Red Sea Project (luxury tourism), Qiddiya (entertainment city)
- **Domestic tourism**: Growing (Saudi nationals traveling within the Kingdom)
- **Women travelers**: Major shift post-2017 (women can now drive, travel without male guardian)

**Payment infrastructure**: mada (domestic card network), STC Pay (mobile wallet), card penetration rising

**Nitaqat (Saudization)**: Companies must hire X% Saudi nationals (varies by industry)

**Aviation**: GCAA (General Civil Aviation Authority), Riyadh Air launching 2024 (new national carrier), Saudi Airlines expansion

**Business culture**: Very hierarchical, relationship-first, conservative (respect Islamic customs)

**Opportunities**: Tourism infrastructure (hotels, experiences), domestic travel (Saudis exploring their own country), women travelers (underserved segment)

## UAE

**Dubai**: Global hub (logistics, finance, tourism). 80% of travel is leisure. High expat population (85% of residents).

**Abu Dhabi**: Government/oil center. Sovereign wealth funds (Mubadala, ADIA) — major investors. Quieter than Dubai but growing tourism (Louvre, F1).

**Free zones**: DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre), ADGM (Abu Dhabi Global Market) — 100% foreign ownership, separate legal systems

**Payment**: High card penetration (credit/debit), BNPL growth (Tabby, Tamara popular), crypto-friendly

**Expat dynamics**: 85% of population is expat (Indians, Filipinos, Pakistanis, Western expats). Remittances = huge market. High turnover (average stay 3-5 years).

**Aviation**: Emirates (premium carrier), Etihad (struggling vs Emirates), FlyDubai (LCC)

**Business culture**: Fast-paced, English widely spoken, mix of Western and Arabic norms, relationship-important but not as critical as Saudi

**Opportunities**: Expat travel (visiting home countries), stopover tourism (Dubai as transit hub), BNPL for travel

## Egypt

**Mass market**: 100M population, lower income levels ($3-5K GDP per capita)

**FX volatility**: Egyptian Pound has devalued repeatedly (2016, 2022) — affects pricing, profitability

**Payment**: Cash-dominant (70%+ of transactions), low card penetration, mobile wallets growing (Vodafone Cash, Orange Money)

**Tourism**: Pyramids, Red Sea resorts (Sharm El-Sheikh, Hurghada), Nile cruises. Heavily dependent on European tourists.

**Government**: Subsidies on fuel/food, import restrictions (affects business operations), bureaucracy

**Business culture**: Relationship-important, slower pace, expect negotiation on everything, patience required

**Opportunities**: Domestic tourism (Egyptians traveling within Egypt), Umrah travel (Egypt to Saudi), outbound (Egyptians traveling to Europe/Gulf)

**Challenges**: FX risk, payment infrastructure, regulatory complexity

## Jordan

**Population**: 10M (small market but educated, diaspora-connected)

**Amman**: Regional HQ for many companies (stable, English-speaking, educated workforce)

**Tourism**: Petra (world wonder), Wadi Rum (desert), Dead Sea, Aqaba (Red Sea access)

**ASEZA** (Aqaba Special Economic Zone): Tax incentives, 0% customs duties

**Syrian refugees**: 1.3M refugees (13% of population) — economic strain but also UNHCR/NGO activity

**Business culture**: Professional, English widely spoken, Westernized compared to GCC

**Opportunities**: Regional HQ location, niche tourism (adventure, archaeology), Jordanian diaspora travel

## GCC Regional Dynamics

**GCC countries**: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman

**Oil price sensitivity**: When oil is $80+, consumer spending is strong. When oil drops below $60, governments cut spending, affects private sector.

**Expatriate populations**: 40-85% of GCC population is expat (highest in UAE/Kuwait). Temporary residency (visas tied to employment).

**Hajj/Umrah seasonality**: Hajj (1 week/year, rotates dates), Umrah (year-round but peak in Ramadan). Massive travel demand to Mecca/Medina.

**Pan-GCC integration**: GCC citizens can travel/work across GCC countries (like Schengen). Ongoing payment integration (GCC Pay).

**Aviation**: Hub competition (Dubai vs Doha vs Riyadh). Emirates vs Qatar Airways vs Etihad. Saudi expanding rapidly (Vision 2030).

**Business culture**: Varies (UAE most liberal, Saudi most conservative, Kuwait in-between). Arabic is official language but English widely used in business.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Treating MENA as one market**: Saudi is not UAE is not Egypt. Segment by country.
2. **Ignoring Ramadan**: Business slows dramatically during Ramadan. Plan around it.
3. **Cold outreach**: Doesn't work. Get warm introductions.
4. **Underestimating relationship-building time**: Expect 6-12 months to build trust before deals close.
5. **Assuming English is enough**: Arabic localization matters (especially in Saudi). RTL (right-to-left) UI, Hijri calendar, prayer time features.
6. **Ignoring women travelers**: Major growth segment in Saudi (post-2017 reforms). Don't overlook.
