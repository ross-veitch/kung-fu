# /market-entry

Build a market entry strategy for a MENA country with regulatory, commercial, and relationship roadmap.

---

## When to use

When a company is considering entering a new MENA market or expanding from one MENA market to another. Produces a phased action plan covering legal setup, commercial strategy, and the relationship-building required to succeed.

---

## Steps

### 1. Market selection rationale
- Why this market? (Market size, growth, strategic fit, existing demand)
- What's the addressable opportunity? (TAM in this specific market)
- Timing: Why now? (Vision 2030 initiatives, regulatory changes, competitor gaps)

### 2. Regulatory and legal setup

**Entity structure options**:
- Local LLC (most common for operational presence)
- Branch office (simpler, but limited activities in some markets)
- Free zone entity (100% foreign ownership, but limited mainland trading)
- Representative office (non-trading, market exploration only)

**Key requirements** (market-specific):
- MISA / investment authority approval
- Trade license and commercial registration
- Saudization / Emiratisation compliance plan
- Data localization requirements
- Payment processing approvals (if fintech-adjacent)

**Timeline**: Estimate realistic setup time (typically 2–6 months depending on market and entity type)

### 3. Commercial strategy

- **Pricing**: How to position relative to local competitors (premium, parity, penetration)
- **Distribution**: Direct (app, website), partnerships, wholesale, B2B
- **Localisation**: Arabic product, local payment methods (mada, Fawry, Tabby/Tamara BNPL), cultural adaptation, RTL UI, Hijri calendar, prayer time considerations
- **Marketing**: Local channels (WhatsApp marketing is dominant in MENA), influencer landscape, local media

### 4. Relationship roadmap
This is the most important section for MENA. Relationships determine success more than product or pricing.

- **Key stakeholders**: Who do you need to know? (Government officials, industry association leaders, potential partners, investors)
- **Warm introductions**: Who in your existing network can introduce you? (Map second-degree connections)
- **Conference and event strategy**: Which events to attend for relationship building (GITEX, LEAP, Arabian Travel Market, STEP, Arabnet)
- **Local partner strategy**: Do you need a local partner? What type? (Distribution partner, JV partner, government relations advisor)
- **Timeline**: Relationship building takes 3–6 months before commercial results. Plan for this.

### 5. Competitive positioning
- How will you differentiate from existing local players?
- What's your unfair advantage in this market?
- What will local competitors do in response to your entry?

### 6. Risk assessment
- **Regulatory risk**: License denial, policy changes, Saudization compliance
- **FX risk**: Especially Egypt (EGP volatility), less concern in GCC (pegged currencies)
- **Geopolitical risk**: Regional tensions, oil price impact on economy
- **Execution risk**: Talent availability, cultural adaptation, timeline slippage

### 7. Phase the plan

- **Phase 1 (Month 1–3)**: Legal setup, initial relationships, market research validation
- **Phase 2 (Month 3–6)**: Soft launch, first partnerships, initial user/client acquisition
- **Phase 3 (Month 6–12)**: Scale operations, expand team, iterate based on market feedback
- **Phase 4 (Month 12+)**: Full operations, evaluate expansion to adjacent MENA markets
