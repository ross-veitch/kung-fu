# /competitive-landscape

Map the competitive landscape for a product or sector in a specific MENA market.

---

## When to use

Before entering a MENA market, evaluating a partnership, or assessing a competitive threat. Produces a structured map of who the players are, how they compete, and where the gaps are.

---

## Steps

### 1. Define scope
- **Market**: Which country? (Saudi, UAE, Egypt, Jordan, or pan-GCC)
- **Sector**: What industry or product category?
- **Segment**: What customer segment? (B2C, B2B, SME, enterprise, government)

### 2. Map the players

For each competitor, capture:
- **Company name and HQ location**
- **Ownership**: Local private, government-linked (GLC/SWF-backed), international, family business
- **Business model**: How do they make money?
- **Scale indicators**: Revenue (if available), headcount, app downloads, web traffic (Similarweb)
- **Funding**: VC-backed? PIF-backed? Self-funded? This reveals growth ambition and runway.
- **Key differentiator**: What's their moat?

Group competitors by tier:
- **Tier 1**: Direct competitors with significant market share
- **Tier 2**: Emerging competitors or adjacent players who could expand
- **Tier 3**: International players not yet in the market but likely to enter

### 3. Analyse competitive dynamics
- **Market concentration**: Is it a fragmented market (many small players) or consolidated (1–2 dominant)?
- **Government influence**: Are any competitors government-backed or benefiting from regulatory preference? (Common in Saudi)
- **Price competition**: Are players competing on price, quality, relationships, or technology?
- **Distribution advantage**: Who has the strongest local distribution (retail presence, partnerships, app penetration)?
- **Localisation depth**: Who has the best Arabic product, local payment integration, and cultural fit?

### 4. Identify gaps and opportunities
- Segments or sub-markets that are underserved
- Technology gaps (e.g., poor mobile experience among incumbents)
- Customer pain points that competitors aren't addressing
- Regulatory advantages for new entrants (e.g., free zone benefits, new licensing categories)

### 5. Assess barriers to entry
- Regulatory requirements (licenses, Saudization, data localization)
- Relationship-dependent business development (wasta factor)
- Capital requirements
- Talent availability
- Brand and trust building timeline
