# /regulatory-check

Check regulatory requirements for an activity in a MENA country. Output: licensing requirements, government approvals needed, local partner requirements, timeline, common obstacles, and when to involve legal counsel.

---

## Step 1: Define the activity

**Activity**: [Operating a travel booking platform / Opening an office / Hiring employees / Processing payments / etc.]  
**Country**: [Saudi Arabia / UAE / Egypt / Jordan]

---

## Step 2: Licensing requirements

**Licenses needed**:
- [License type 1]: [What it allows, cost, timeline]
- [License type 2]: [What it allows, cost, timeline]

**Example (Saudi Arabia, travel booking platform)**:
- **Tourism license** (SCTH): Required to operate travel services. Cost: $5K. Timeline: 2-3 months.
- **Payment license** (SAMA): Required if processing payments locally. Cost: $50K+. Timeline: 6-12 months.

---

## Step 3: Foreign ownership rules

**Free zone vs mainland**:
- **Free zone** (DIFC, ADGM, etc.): 100% foreign ownership, separate legal system, fast setup (2-4 weeks)
- **Mainland**: May require local partner (51% Emirati in UAE mainland), full access to local market

**Saudi Arabia**: 100% foreign ownership allowed in most sectors (Vision 2030 incentive)  
**Egypt**: Varies by sector (tourism: 100% allowed, retail: 51% max)

---

## Step 4: Government approvals

**What approvals needed**:
- [Ministry/agency 1]: [What they approve, timeline]
- [Ministry/agency 2]: [What they approve, timeline]

**Example**:
- **GCAA (Saudi)**: Approval for travel platform that sells flights. Timeline: 1-2 months.
- **SCTH (Saudi)**: Tourism license approval. Timeline: 2-3 months.

---

## Step 5: Timeline and obstacles

**Total timeline**: [X months]

**Common obstacles**:
- Documentation requirements (notarized, translated, apostilled)
- Bureaucracy (slow approvals, multiple agencies)
- Local partner negotiations (if required)
- Capital requirements (min capital to register company)

---

## Output Example

```markdown
# Regulatory Check: Operating Travel Booking Platform in Saudi Arabia

## Licenses Required

1. **Tourism License** (SCTH)
   - **Purpose**: Operate travel booking services
   - **Cost**: SAR 20K (~$5K USD)
   - **Timeline**: 2-3 months

2. **Payment License** (SAMA) — **Optional**
   - **Purpose**: Process payments locally (can use 3rd party payment gateway instead)
   - **Cost**: SAR 200K+ (~$50K USD)
   - **Timeline**: 6-12 months
   - **Recommendation**: Use existing payment provider (STC Pay, HyperPay) to avoid this

## Foreign Ownership

- **100% foreign ownership allowed** (Vision 2030 incentive for tourism sector)
- **No local partner required**

## Government Approvals

1. **MISA** (Ministry of Investment): Company registration approval (1 month)
2. **SCTH** (Saudi Commission for Tourism & Heritage): Tourism license approval (2-3 months)
3. **GCAA** (if selling flights): Aviation services approval (1-2 months)

## Timeline

**Total**: 4-6 months from application to launch

## Common Obstacles

- **Documentation**: All docs must be notarized + translated to Arabic
- **SCTH approval**: Can be slow; relationship with SCTH speeds this up
- **Nitaqat compliance**: Must hire X% Saudi nationals (starts at 10% for small companies)

## Recommendation

- **Start company registration immediately** (MISA approval takes 1 month)
- **Engage local legal counsel** (Al-Jadaan or Clyde & Co in Riyadh)
- **Use 3rd party payment** (avoid SAMA license complexity)
```
